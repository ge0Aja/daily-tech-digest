## AI & Machine Learning

### NVIDIA Launches Ising: Open-Source AI Models for Quantum Computing

**Source:** NVIDIA Newsroom | [Read more](https://nvidianews.nvidia.com/news/nvidia-launches-ising-the-worlds-first-open-ai-models-to-accelerate-the-path-to-useful-quantum-computers)

Announced on World Quantum Day (April 14), NVIDIA's Ising is the world's first family of open-source AI models for quantum error correction and calibration. The suite includes Ising Calibration, a vision-language model that interprets quantum processor measurements, and Ising Decoding, 3D CNN variants for real-time error correction. Ising delivers up to 2.5x faster performance and 3x higher accuracy than existing open-source tools, reducing quantum processor calibration time from days to hours — a key step toward fault-tolerant quantum computers.

## Security & Privacy

### Microsoft Patch Tuesday April 2026: 167 Flaws, SharePoint Zero-Day Actively Exploited

**Source:** BleepingComputer | [Read more](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/microsoft/microsoft-april-2026-patch-tuesday-fixes-167-flaws-2-zero-days/)

Microsoft's April Patch Tuesday addressed 167 vulnerabilities including 2 zero-days. CVE-2026-32201, a SharePoint Server spoofing vulnerability caused by improper input validation, is actively exploited in the wild and was added to CISA's KEV catalog with a federal remediation deadline of April 28. The batch also covers critical RCE flaws in Windows TCP/IP, Active Directory, IKE, Word, and Remote Desktop Client. Organizations running SharePoint 2016, 2019, or Subscription Edition should treat this as an emergency patch.

### Chrome Zero-Day CVE-2026-5281 (WebGPU Dawn): Chrome's Fourth 0-Day of 2026

**Source:** Security Affairs | [Read more](https://securityaffairs.com/190265/hacking/google-fixes-fourth-actively-exploited-chrome-zero-day-of-2026.html)

Google patched CVE-2026-5281, a use-after-free in Dawn — Chrome's WebGPU implementation — in Chrome 146.0.7680.177/178 for all platforms. A renderer-compromised attacker can exploit this for arbitrary code execution; in advanced attack chains, it can break Chrome's sandbox entirely. This is Chrome's fourth actively exploited zero-day in 2026. CISA added it to the KEV catalog on April 1 with a federal patch deadline of April 15; all teams relying on browser-based interfaces should verify automated updates are functioning.

### Fortinet FortiClient EMS Pre-Auth SQL Injection (CVE-2026-21643, CVSS 9.8) Under Active Attack

**Source:** Help Net Security | [Read more](https://www.helpnetsecurity.com/2026/03/30/forticlient-ems-cve-2026-21643-reported-exploitation/)

A pre-authentication SQL injection in FortiClient EMS 7.4.4 is actively exploited via the public `/api/v1/init_consts` endpoint, which has no lockout and returns verbose database errors — enabling rapid data extraction from multi-tenant deployments with zero credentials. CISA added CVE-2026-21643 to its KEV catalog on April 13, requiring federal agencies to patch by April 16. Organizations should upgrade to FortiClient EMS 7.4.5 or later immediately.

### Fake Ledger Live App on Apple Mac App Store Steals $9.5M in Crypto

**Source:** BleepingComputer | [Read more](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/fake-ledger-live-app-on-apples-app-store-stole-95m-in-crypto/)

A fraudulent Ledger Live app was live on the Mac App Store for approximately two weeks, stealing ~$9.5 million from 50+ victims between April 7–13 by tricking users into entering crypto wallet seed phrases. Three individual victims each lost over $1 million; stolen funds were laundered through 150+ wallet addresses via a mixing service on KuCoin. The incident exposes ongoing gaps in Apple's App Store review process for apps impersonating established security tools and is a reminder that legitimate hardware wallet software is never distributed through app stores.

**Source:** VRT NWS | [Read more](https://www.vrt.be/vrtnws/en/2026/04/13/customer-data-hacked-both-at-basic-fit-and-booking-com/)

## Systems & Engineering

### Apache Iceberg v3 in Public Preview on Databricks — Deletion Vectors Deliver 10x DML Speedup

**Source:** Databricks Blog | [Read more](https://www.databricks.com/blog/next-era-open-lakehouse-apache-icebergtm-v3-public-preview-databricks)

Databricks put Apache Iceberg v3 into public preview with Runtime 18.0+, shipping Deletion Vectors (up to 10x faster DML vs. copy-on-write), Row Lineage (permanent row IDs for data tracking), and a VARIANT type for semi-structured data processing. A new efficient column-update design targets AI/ML workloads with wide tables containing embedding vectors and model scores, enabling partial-column writes instead of full file rewrites. Snowflake also added Iceberg v3 preview support simultaneously, signaling broad ecosystem convergence on the open table format.

### EU AI Act Fully Enforceable August 2026: DMA Fines Hit Apple (€500M) and Meta (€200M)

**Source:** Dataconomy | [Read more](https://dataconomy.com/2026/04/15/eu-ai-act-is-strangling-european-innovation/)

The EU AI Act becomes fully enforceable on August 2, 2026, and digital regulation is already producing major consequences: Apple was fined €500M and Meta €200M for DMA non-compliance in April, while Google excluded EEA, Switzerland, and UK users from its new Gemini Personal Intelligence feature citing regulatory barriers. The EU Commission's "Digital Omnibus" proposal threatens to weaken both the AI Act and GDPR protections, facing pushback from civil rights groups. Engineering teams shipping AI products to EU users need high-risk AI system assessments and compliance documentation in place before August.
