### NVIDIA Ising: World's First Open AI Models for Quantum Error Correction
**Source:** NVIDIA Newsroom | [Read more](https://nvidianews.nvidia.com/news/nvidia-launches-ising-the-worlds-first-open-ai-models-to-accelerate-the-path-to-useful-quantum-computers)

NVIDIA launched Ising on April 14—the first open AI model family targeting quantum computing's two hardest engineering bottlenecks: processor calibration and real-time error correction decoding. Ising Calibration is a vision-language model for automated continuous tuning of quantum processors; Ising Decoding uses 3D convolutional neural networks for real-time error correction at 2.5x faster speed and 3x higher accuracy than prior open-source tools. Models are available on GitHub, Hugging Face, and build.nvidia.com and are already in use at national labs and quantum computing startups.

**Source:** Bleeping Computer | [Read more](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/microsoft/microsoft-april-2026-patch-tuesday-fixes-167-flaws-2-zero-days/)

Microsoft's April 2026 Patch Tuesday addressed 163–168 CVEs including two zero-days. CVE-2026-32201 (SharePoint Server spoofing, CVSS 6.5) requires no authentication and no user interaction, allows attackers to view and tamper with sensitive data, and is actively exploited in the wild—CISA added it to the Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog with a federal remediation deadline of April 28. ### Adobe Acrobat Reader Zero-Day Exploited Since November 2025 (CVE-2026-34621)
**Source:** Help Net Security | [Read more](https://www.helpnetsecurity.com/2026/04/13/adobe-acrobat-reader-cve-2026-34621-emergency-fix/)

Adobe issued an emergency patch for CVE-2026-34621 (CVSS 9.6), a critical prototype pollution vulnerability in Acrobat Reader that enables arbitrary code execution when a user opens a crafted PDF. The flaw was silently exploited in the wild for five months before being discovered via EXPMON; attack samples used Russian oil-and-gas themed lures, suggesting APT involvement. Users on Acrobat DC, Reader DC, and Acrobat 2024 across Windows and macOS should update immediately to the versions released April 13.

## Industry & Trends

### Open Source AI in 2026: Licensing Ambiguity and "Open Washing" Risk
**Source:** Linux Insider | [Read more](https://www.linuxinsider.com/story/open-source-in-2026-faces-a-defining-moment-177630.html)

Open source AI enters 2026 under mounting scrutiny as licensing disputes intensify. OSI's formal "open source AI" definition requires free use, study, modification, and sharing—but Meta's Llama family withholds training data and imposes proprietary restrictions, failing OSI's criteria despite broad marketing as "open." Confusing open-weight with open-source creates real legal and compliance exposure. With EU AI Act enforcement underway and U.S. state regulators accelerating, engineers must scrutinize model licenses carefully before production deployment.

### U.S. AI Regulation Surge: 600+ Bills, NIST Agent Standards, DOJ AI Task Force
**Source:** Inside Global Tech | [Read more](https://www.insideglobaltech.com/2026/04/06/u-s-tech-legislative-regulatory-update-first-quarter-2026/)
