## AI & Machine Learning

### GLM-5.1: Open-Source LLM Tops Coding Benchmarks
**Z.ai (Zhipu AI)** | [Read more](https://venturebeat.com/technology/ai-joins-the-8-hour-work-day-as-glm-ships-5-1-open-source-llm-beating-opus-4)

Z.ai released GLM-5.1 on April 7 — a 744B-parameter Mixture-of-Experts model under the MIT license that scored 58.4 on SWE-Bench Pro, edging past GPT-5.4 (57.7) and Claude Opus 4.6 (57.3). With 40B active parameters and a 200K token context window, it autonomously runs "plan-execute-test-fix" loops for up to 8 hours without human intervention. Remarkably, it was trained entirely on domestic Chinese compute despite US chip export controls on Nvidia hardware, demonstrating that those restrictions haven't prevented frontier-class AI development.

**The Hacker News** | [Read more](https://thehackernews.com/2026/04/anthropics-claude-mythos-finds.html)

## Security & Privacy

### Mercor AI Startup Hit by LiteLLM Supply Chain Attack — 4TB of Data Stolen
**TechCrunch** | [Read more](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/31/mercor-says-it-was-hit-by-cyberattack-tied-to-compromise-of-open-source-litellm-project/)

TeamPCP planted malicious code in LiteLLM — an open-source library downloaded millions of times daily for connecting apps to AI APIs — affecting thousands of companies including Mercor, a $10B AI training-data startup serving OpenAI, Anthropic, and Meta. Lapsus$ claims to have exfiltrated 4TB of data including source code and internal records; 40,000+ individuals' PII may be exposed and a class action was filed within days. Mercor confirmed it was "one of thousands" impacted, making this a stark warning about supply-chain risk in the rapidly growing AI tooling ecosystem.

### Microsoft: AI-Enabled OAuth Device Code Phishing Now Bypasses MFA
**Microsoft Security Blog** | [Read more](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/security/blog/2026/04/06/ai-enabled-device-code-phishing-campaign-april-2026/)

Threat actors are exploiting OAuth device code authentication flows with AI-driven automation, dynamically generating codes at click-time to bypass the 15-minute expiration window — effectively nullifying MFA protections. Attacks use serverless infrastructure and AI-generated hyper-personalized lures, leading to organizational account compromise and financial data exfiltration at scale. Organizations should audit and disable unnecessary device code flows and adopt phishing-resistant authentication (FIDO2/passkeys).

### CISA Flags 7 Actively Exploited CVEs in Fortinet, Microsoft, and Adobe
**The Hacker News** | [Read more](https://thehackernews.com/2026/04/cisa-adds-6-known-exploited-flaws-in.html)

CISA added 7 CVEs to its Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog on April 13, including a CVSS 9.1 SQL injection in Fortinet FortiClient EMS (CVE-2026-21643), a CVSS 8.8 deserialization RCE in Microsoft Exchange Server (CVE-2023-21529), and a use-after-free in Adobe Acrobat Reader. Federal agencies must patch by April 27; FortiClient EMS patches were required by April 16. All three products are widely deployed enterprise software, making rapid patching essential for any organization running these systems.

## Cloud & Infrastructure

### AWS Interconnect for Multicloud Now GA — Google Cloud as First Partner
**AWS** | [Read more](https://aws.amazon.com/about-aws/whats-new/2026/04/aws-announces-ga-AWS-interconnect-multicloud/)

AWS announced general availability of AWS Interconnect – multicloud, enabling private, MACsec-encrypted, high-speed connections between Amazon VPCs and other cloud providers, with Google Cloud as launch partner (Azure and OCI to follow later in 2026). Available in five region pairs across the US and Europe, it integrates with Transit Gateway and Cloud WAN using an open networking specification any provider can adopt. With 89% of enterprises running multicloud, this significantly lowers the cost and complexity of secure cross-cloud private networking, eliminating the need for expensive third-party interconnect providers.

## Systems & Engineering

### Agentic AI Is Forcing a Rethink of Wide-Area Network Architecture
**SiliconANGLE** | [Read more](https://siliconangle.com/2026/04/10/agentic-ai-will-force-rethink-network-edge/)

Agentic AI systems — autonomous agents collaborating in real time across distributed environments — are exposing fundamental mismatches with current WAN architectures designed for human-driven request-response patterns. New requirements include ultra-low latency for inter-agent communication, deterministic routing, and edge compute proximity, demanding a rethink of enterprise network edge design. Engineers building distributed agent systems should factor in these networking constraints during orchestration topology design.

### China's SAMR Platform Pricing Rules Take Effect April 2026
**Global Policy Watch** | [Read more](https://www.globalpolicywatch.com/2026/04/u-s-tech-legislative-regulatory-update-first-quarter-2026/)

China's State Administration for Market Regulation activated rules prohibiting internet platforms from imposing forced discounts, price-matching obligations, or using data and algorithms to price-discriminate against consumers or business users.
