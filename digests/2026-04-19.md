## Security & Privacy

### TeamPCP Supply Chain Attack Backdoors LiteLLM via Compromised Trivy CI/CD
**Source:** The Hacker News / Datadog Security Labs | [Read more](https://thehackernews.com/2026/03/teampcp-backdoors-litellm-versions.html)

Threat actor TeamPCP compromised Aqua Security's Trivy vulnerability scanner to harvest CI/CD secrets, then published backdoored LiteLLM versions 1.82.7 and 1.82.8 to PyPI. The payload — active for ~40 minutes before PyPI quarantine — harvested SSH keys, AWS/GCP/Azure credentials, Kubernetes tokens, and deployed a persistent systemd backdoor (`sysmon.service`) with Kubernetes lateral movement capabilities. LiteLLM sees ~3.4M daily downloads; any project that imported `litellm.proxy` on March 24 during that window should treat the environment as compromised and rotate all credentials immediately.

**Source:** IBM Newsroom | [Read more](https://newsroom.ibm.com/2026-04-15-ibm-announces-new-cybersecurity-measures-to-help-enterprises-confront-agentic-attacks)

### AI Agents Independently Discover Chained RCE Vulnerabilities in CUPS Print Server
**Source:** The Register | [Read more](https://www.theregister.com/2026/04/06/ai_agents_cups_server_rce/)

AI agents discovered two chained CVEs (CVE-2026-34980 and CVE-2026-34990) in CUPS — the standard print server on Linux and Unix — allowing a low-privileged account to achieve root file overwrite. The disclosure highlights a dual-use dynamic: LLM-driven tooling accelerates vulnerability discovery for defenders, but also dramatically lowers the bar for exploit development since models can rapidly convert technical writeups into working proof-of-concept code. Admins running CUPS should apply patches immediately.

### EU AI Act Becomes Fully Enforceable August 2, 2026 — Weakening Omnibus Proposal in Dispute
**Source:** Artificial Intelligence Act EU / TechPolicy.Press | [Read more](https://artificialintelligenceact.eu/)

The EU AI Act reaches full enforceability on August 2, 2026, imposing compliance obligations on organizations deploying high-risk AI systems in hiring, healthcare, and law enforcement — including mandatory impact assessments, human-in-the-loop controls, and audit logging. The European Commission's AI Omnibus package — framed as a simplification measure — has drawn opposition from 40+ human rights organizations who argue it weakens protections against biometric identification and surveillance AI. Engineering and product teams shipping AI in the EU need to finalize compliance infrastructure now.

### MIT Technology Review: AI Is Redefining What Software Engineering Means
**Source:** MIT Technology Review | [Read more](https://www.technologyreview.com/2026/04/14/1134397/redefining-the-future-of-software-engineering/)

An April 14 MIT Tech Review piece argues that the fundamental nature of software engineering is shifting: developers are spending less time writing code and more time orchestrating, directing, and reviewing output from AI coding agents.
