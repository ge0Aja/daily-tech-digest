## Security & Privacy

### Microsoft Defender Triple Zero-Days: BlueHammer, RedSun, and UnDefend Exploited in the Wild
**Source:** The Hacker News | [Read more](https://thehackernews.com/2026/04/three-microsoft-defender-zero-days.html)

Three zero-days in Windows Defender — BlueHammer (CVE-2026-33825), RedSun, and UnDefend — have been chained in active attacks since April 10. BlueHammer and RedSun are TOCTOU local privilege escalation bugs that exploit Defender's privileged file operations to gain SYSTEM; UnDefend then blinds Defender by corrupting definition updates over time. Microsoft patched BlueHammer in April's Patch Tuesday; RedSun and UnDefend remain unpatched — affected hosts should be isolated immediately.

### Adobe Acrobat Reader Zero-Day CVE-2026-34621: Five Months Undetected
**Source:** Help Net Security | [Read more](https://www.helpnetsecurity.com/2026/04/09/acrobat-reader-zero-day-exploited/)

CVE-2026-34621, a prototype pollution bug in Acrobat Reader (CVSS 8.6), executes malicious JavaScript the moment a victim opens a crafted PDF — no further interaction required. The flaw had been exploited in the wild since November 2025, five months before Adobe's emergency patch landed on April 12. CISA added it to the KEV catalog requiring federal agencies to patch by April 27. All users should update to Acrobat DC v26.001.21411 or Acrobat 2024 immediately.

## Cloud & Infrastructure

### AWS Interconnect Multicloud Now Generally Available with Google Cloud
**Source:** AWS | [Read more](https://aws.amazon.com/blogs/aws/aws-interconnect-is-now-generally-available-with-a-new-option-to-simplify-last-mile-connectivity/)

AWS Interconnect Multicloud — now GA — delivers Layer 3 private connections between AWS VPCs and partner clouds over the AWS global backbone with MACsec encryption, never touching the public internet. ### GKE Two-Step Control Plane Upgrades with Rollback Safety Now in Preview
**Source:** Google Cloud | [Read more](https://cloud.google.com/blog/topics/inside-google-cloud/whats-new-google-cloud-2025)

Google Kubernetes Engine now offers two-step control plane minor version upgrades in public preview, giving platform teams a validation window before committing a new Kubernetes version cluster-wide. If issues emerge post-upgrade, operators can roll back to the previous control plane minor version before changes propagate to nodes. This meaningfully reduces the blast radius of GKE version upgrades for production clusters.

### EU Cyber Resilience Act: September 2026 Reporting Obligations Now Imminent
**Source:** European Commission | [Read more](https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/cyber-resilience-act)

The EU CRA's security incident reporting obligations go live September 11, 2026 — organizations must actively report exploited vulnerabilities and severe incidents affecting products with digital elements. The CRA scopes roughly 76% of OSS across every product supply chain; only non-monetized open-source projects qualify for exemption. OSS stewards must establish a cybersecurity policy and cooperate with market surveillance authorities, though they are shielded from administrative fines. Full manufacturer compliance obligations follow in December 2027.

### US States Roll Out 600+ AI Bills in Q1 2026 as DOJ Creates AI Litigation Task Force
**Source:** Global Policy Watch | [Read more](https://www.globalpolicywatch.com/2026/04/u-s-tech-legislative-regulatory-update-first-quarter-2026/)

Over 600 AI bills were introduced in US state legislatures in Q1 2026, with Indiana, Utah, and Washington enacting laws barring insurers from using AI as the sole basis for denying claims. The DOJ stood up an AI Litigation Task Force to challenge state AI laws that conflict with interstate commerce or federal regulation. The widening patchwork of state-level rules versus federal inaction is creating significant compliance complexity for engineering teams building AI products across US jurisdictions.
