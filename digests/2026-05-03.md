### MIT's FTTE Framework Slashes Federated Learning Memory Use by 80%
**Source:** MIT News | **URL:** https://news.mit.edu/2026/enabling-privacy-preserving-ai-training-everyday-devices-0429

MIT researchers developed FTTE (Federated Tiny Training Engine), enabling privacy-preserving AI training on constrained edge devices like smartwatches and sensors without centralizing data. It reduces on-device memory by 80%, cuts communication payload by 69%, and converges in far fewer rounds than synchronous baselines — validated on networks with up to 500 clients and 90% stragglers. The key techniques are sparse parameter updates and staleness-weighted aggregation to handle device heterogeneity.

## Security & Privacy

### Critical cPanel Zero-Day Exploited Two Months Before Patch (CVE-2026-41940)
**Source:** Help Net Security | **URL:** https://www.helpnetsecurity.com/2026/04/30/cpanel-zero-day-vulnerability-cve-2026-41940-exploited/

A critical authentication bypass in cPanel and WHM (CVE-2026-41940, CVSS 9.8) was actively exploited as a zero-day from approximately February 23 — two months before an emergency patch was issued on April 28. The flaw chains a CRLF injection in the session writer with a malformed cookie to bypass authentication and gain full administrative access without credentials. Roughly 1.5 million cPanel instances are exposed online; a public PoC is now available and exploitation is ongoing.

**Source:** BankInfoSecurity | **URL:** https://www.bankinfosecurity.com/medtronic-already-facing-federal-lawsuits-in-recent-hack-a-31570

### CISA Adds Linux Kernel Privilege Escalation to Known Exploited Vulnerabilities
**Source:** CISA | **URL:** https://www.cisa.gov/news-events/alerts/2026/05/01/cisa-adds-one-known-exploited-vulnerability-catalog

CISA added CVE-2026-31431 — a Linux kernel incorrect resource transfer vulnerability (CVSS 7.8) — to its Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog on May 1, mandating remediation deadlines for federal agencies. The flaw allows an unprivileged local user to escalate privileges to root. In April, CISA also catalogued 8 actively exploited flaws affecting Cisco, SonicWall, and ASUS devices, underscoring a steady cadence of critical infrastructure vulnerabilities.

## Cloud & Infrastructure

### Kubernetes GPU Utilization Averages Just 5% as AI Workloads Drive Massive Waste
**Source:** Cast AI | **URL:** https://cast.ai/press-release/2026-state-of-kubernetes-optimization-report/

Cast AI's 2026 State of Kubernetes Optimization Report, analyzing 23,000 clusters, finds GPU utilization averaging just 5% — meaning organizations provision roughly 20x more GPU capacity than they actively consume. CPU utilization dropped further to 8% (down from 10% a year earlier), while cloud vendors raised H200 GPU prices 15%, breaking a 20-year cost-reduction trend. Advanced GPU scheduling can push utilization past 80%, but few clusters have adopted it.
