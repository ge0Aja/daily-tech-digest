## Security & Privacy

### CVE-2026-31431 "Copy Fail": Linux Kernel Root Privilege Escalation Added to CISA KEV
**Source:** Microsoft Security Blog | **URL:** https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/security/blog/2026/05/01/cve-2026-31431-copy-fail-vulnerability-enables-linux-root-privilege-escalation/

CVE-2026-31431 is a CVSS 7.8 local privilege escalation flaw in the Linux kernel's cryptographic subsystem that allows any unprivileged user to achieve full root access. The exploit is deterministic (no race conditions required), fits in approximately 732 bytes, and affects virtually all major Linux distributions running kernels from 2017 onward — including Ubuntu 24.04 LTS, RHEL 10.1, Amazon Linux 2023, and SUSE 16. In Kubernetes and cloud multi-tenant environments, successful exploitation can enable container breakout. Engineers should patch immediately or disable AF_ALG sockets as an interim mitigation.

### CVE-2026-41940: cPanel/WHM Authentication Bypass Exploited as Zero-Day for Two Months
**Source:** Help Net Security | **URL:** https://www.helpnetsecurity.com/2026/04/30/cpanel-zero-day-vulnerability-cve-2026-41940-exploited/

A CRLF injection in cPanel and WHM's session handling (CVE-2026-41940, CVSS 9.8) allowed unauthenticated remote attackers to inject arbitrary properties — including `user=root` — into session files, bypassing authentication entirely and gaining root-level admin access. Approximately 1.5 million internet-exposed cPanel instances were vulnerable; exploitation was observed from February 23, roughly two months before the emergency patch released April 28 (fixed in v11.136.0.5). CISA added it to the Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog with a federal remediation deadline of May 3, 2026.

**Source:** SecurityWeek | **URL:** https://www.securityweek.com/microsoft-patches-exploited-sharepoint-zero-day-and-160-other-vulnerabilities/

Microsoft's April 2026 Patch Tuesday addressed 169 CVEs including an actively exploited SharePoint spoofing zero-day (CVE-2026-32201, CVSS 6.5). ### Mandiant M-Trends 2026: Mean Time to Exploit Is Now Negative 7 Days
**Source:** Google Cloud Blog | **URL:** https://cloud.google.com/blog/topics/threat-intelligence/m-trends-2026

Mandiant's M-Trends 2026 report, drawing on 450,000 hours of incident response, found that the mean time to exploit vulnerabilities dropped to -7 days — meaning exploitation now routinely precedes patch releases. Attackers are handing off compromised network access in as little as 22 seconds, down from over 8 hours in 2022, and exploits have remained the top initial access vector for six consecutive years. Internet-facing enterprise applications (SAP NetWeaver, Oracle E-Business Suite) are primary targets, with zero-day exploitation outpacing n-day exploitation among the top CVEs in 2025.

## Industry & Trends

### EU Cyber Resilience Act: First Enforcement Deadlines Arrive This Summer
**Source:** EU Digital Strategy | **URL:** https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/cyber-resilience-act

The EU Cyber Resilience Act's first enforcement milestones land in 2026: type A horizontal products must comply by August 30, CRA reporting requirements apply from September 11, and full requirements take effect December 11, 2027. Commercial products that integrate open-source components must meet supply-chain security standards even though purely community-driven unmonetiized OSS remains exempt. Engineers and product teams shipping software with embedded OSS into EU markets should be auditing component provenance and security practices now, as non-compliance creates direct legal liability for integrators.
