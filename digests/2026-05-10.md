### Five Eyes Agencies Issue First Joint Agentic AI Security Guidance
_CISA_ | [Read more](https://www.cisa.gov/resources-tools/resources/careful-adoption-agentic-ai-services)

Six agencies (CISA, NSA, ASD ACSC, Canadian CCCS, NZ NCSC, UK NCSC) jointly published "Careful Adoption of Agentic AI Services" — the first coordinated international advisory on autonomous AI agents. The guidance warns that agentic systems inside critical infrastructure are being granted excessive access without adequate monitoring, and explicitly states organizations "should assume that agentic AI systems may behave unexpectedly." Recommendations span five risk categories (privilege, design, behavioral, structural, supply-chain) and call for least-privilege controls, incremental deployment starting with low-risk tasks, and mandatory human oversight until evaluation standards mature.

## Security & Privacy

### CVE-2026-31431 "Copy Fail": Nine-Year-Old Linux Kernel Flaw Under Active Exploitation
_Microsoft Security Blog / Wiz_ | [Read more](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/security/blog/2026/05/01/cve-2026-31431-copy-fail-vulnerability-enables-linux-root-privilege-escalation/)

A logic flaw in the Linux kernel's `algif_aead` cryptographic module (AF_ALG API) allows any unprivileged local user to write 4 controlled bytes into the kernel page cache of any readable file — enabling reliable root privilege escalation (CVSS 7.8). The bug affects virtually every major Linux distribution (Ubuntu, RHEL, SUSE, Debian, Amazon Linux) running kernels from 2017 onward and also enables container escape in Kubernetes environments. A public PoC works across distributions in 732 bytes; CISA added it to KEV with a federal deadline of May 15. Apply kernel patches immediately or restrict AF_ALG socket creation as an interim mitigation.

### CVE-2026-0300: Critical Palo Alto PAN-OS Zero-Day Under Active Exploitation
_Help Net Security / Bleeping Computer_ | [Read more](https://www.helpnetsecurity.com/2026/05/06/palo-alto-firewalls-vulnerability-exploited-cve-2026-0300/)

A buffer overflow in the PAN-OS User-ID Authentication Portal allows unauthenticated remote attackers to execute arbitrary code with full root privileges (CVSS 9.3) on PA-Series and VM-Series firewalls. No credentials, user interaction, or special conditions are required. CISA added CVE-2026-0300 to KEV on May 6 with a federal deadline of May 9; first patches arrive May 13. Until then, restrict Captive Portal access to trusted internal IPs or disable the feature entirely if not in use.

### AWS and Google Cloud Launch Joint Multicloud Networking Standard
_InfoQ_ | [Read more](https://www.infoq.com/news/2025/12/aws-gcp-multicloud-networking/)

AWS and Google Cloud previewed a common standard for secure multicloud networking, with Azure expected to join in 2026. ## Industry & Trends

### Colorado AI Act Takes Effect June 30: Obligations for Developers of High-Risk AI
_Brownstein / Seyfarth_ | [Read more](https://www.bhfs.com/insight/colorados-landmark-ai-law-coming-online-what-developers-and-deployers-should-know/)

Colorado's Artificial Intelligence Act (SB 24-205) takes effect June 30, 2026 after a delayed rollout. Developers of "high-risk" AI systems — covering hiring, lending, housing, healthcare, and education — must document training data, evaluation methodology, data governance measures, bias mitigation steps, and intended outputs, and must produce this documentation for regulators on request. Failure to use "reasonable care" to prevent algorithmic discrimination carries legal liability. Engineers building or deploying AI in covered domains should audit compliance posture before the deadline.
