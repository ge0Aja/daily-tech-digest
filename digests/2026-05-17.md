### Five Eyes & CISA Issue First-Ever Joint Agentic AI Security Guidance
_CISA_ | [Read more](https://www.cisa.gov/resources-tools/resources/careful-adoption-of-agentic-ai-services)

On May 1, six national cybersecurity agencies (CISA, NSA, plus Australian, Canadian, New Zealand, and UK cyber agencies) published a 30-page joint document titled "Careful Adoption of Agentic AI Services" — the first coordinated Five Eyes policy targeting a single AI attack surface. The guidance identifies five risk categories: over-privileged access, design/configuration flaws, unpredictable agent behavior, structural cascading failures, and supply chain risks. Core recommendations apply established principles — zero trust, defense-in-depth, least-privilege — directly to AI agent deployments. The central warning: agents capable of taking real-world actions are already deployed in critical infrastructure, and most organizations grant them far more access than they can safely monitor or control.

## Security & Privacy

### Microsoft Exchange Zero-Day CVE-2026-42897 Exploited in the Wild, Added to CISA KEV
_Help Net Security_ | [Read more](https://www.helpnetsecurity.com/2026/05/15/exchange-server-cve-2026-42897-exploited/)

CVE-2026-42897 is a spoofing flaw rooted in a cross-site scripting vulnerability (CVSS 8.1) affecting on-premises Exchange Server 2016, 2019, and Subscription Edition. An attacker sends a crafted email that, when opened in Outlook Web Access under specific interaction conditions, executes arbitrary JavaScript in the victim's browser context. CISA added it to the Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog on May 15, with a federal agency remediation deadline of May 29. Organizations with Exchange Emergency Mitigation Service enabled (the default) have already received automatic mitigation; administrators who have disabled EM must apply the patch manually and immediately.

### Instructure Canvas LMS Breach: 9,000 Schools, 275 Million Users Exposed
_Security Affairs_ | [Read more](https://securityaffairs.com/191686/cyber-crime/educational-tech-firm-instructure-data-breach-may-have-impacted-9000-schools.html)

ShinyHunters exploited a production system vulnerability at Instructure on April 30, exfiltrating 3.65 TB of data from Canvas LMS and affecting roughly 9,000 universities, K-12 schools, and educational ministries worldwide — including Harvard, MIT, and Oxford. Stolen data includes names, email addresses, student IDs, and private messages; Instructure reports no passwords, government IDs, or financial records were taken. The breach hit during final exam periods, causing widespread operational disruption for affected institutions. This is the largest educational data breach on record and highlights systemic risk in centralized SaaS platforms where a single compromise exposes hundreds of millions of users across thousands of organizations simultaneously.

### Microsoft SharePoint CVE-2026-32201: Unauthenticated RCE with Active Lateral Movement to AD
_SecurityWeek_ | [Read more](https://www.securityweek.com/microsoft-patches-exploited-sharepoint-zero-day-and-160-other-vulnerabilities/)

CVE-2026-32201 is a critical zero-day in on-premises SharePoint Server enabling unauthenticated remote code execution via crafted HTTP requests, patched by Microsoft in April alongside 160+ other vulnerabilities. Threat actors observed in active campaigns pivot from initial SharePoint access to ADFS, Azure AD Connect, and file shares within hours, establishing persistence through scheduled tasks and IIS HTTP modules that survive standard reimaging. CISA's KEV remediation deadline was April 28; over 1,300 servers remain exposed as of May. Given the demonstrated post-exploitation path to full identity infrastructure compromise, organizations running on-premises SharePoint should treat this as an emergency-priority remediation regardless of network exposure level.

### Kubernetes Long-Term Support Debate Intensifies as Regulated Cloud Deployments Scale
_Cloud Native Now_ | [Read more](https://cloudnativenow.com/contributed-content/why-longer-kubernetes-release-cycles-are-critical-for-private-cloud-adoption/)

The Kubernetes community faces growing pressure to introduce a formal Long-Term Support release model as adoption accelerates in classified, financial, and healthcare environments. Current upstream releases carry a 12-month support window, but downstream distributions add 6–12 months of lag, creating a "support gap" that forces regulated organizations to either run behind on security patches or invest heavily in backporting. Gartner projects $80.4 billion in sovereign cloud spending in 2026 — a 35% year-over-year increase — the majority in environments requiring LTS-style stability guarantees. Whether the CNCF formalizes LTS releases or leaves it to vendors will have material impact on enterprise platform engineering roadmaps over the next two to three years.

### 2026 State of Open Source: Vendor Lock-in Avoidance Up 68%; AI Code Generation Enables Copyleft Bypass
_Open Source Initiative_ | [Read more](https://opensource.org/blog/the-2026-state-of-open-source-report)

The 2026 State of Open Source Report finds vendor lock-in avoidance is the primary driver of OSS adoption, cited by 55% of respondents — a 68% year-over-year increase. Simultaneously, AI model licensing is fragmenting the ecosystem: most "open-weight" models don't meet OSI's open source definition (training data and full code not disclosed), and AI code generation tools are enabling companies to rapidly rewrite GPL/AGPL codebases, effectively bypassing copyleft protections at a fraction of historical cost. At enterprises with 5,000+ employees, 60% of engineers spend more than half their time on maintenance rather than features. The industry faces a reckoning on sustainable OSS funding models as reliance deepens and investment lags.
