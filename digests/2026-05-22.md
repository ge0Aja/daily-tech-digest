### Five Eyes Agencies Issue First Joint Agentic AI Security Guidance
**Source:** CISA | [Read more](https://www.cisa.gov/resources-tools/resources/careful-adoption-agentic-ai-services)

CISA and cybersecurity agencies from all Five Eyes nations (US, UK, Australia, Canada, New Zealand) jointly published "Careful Adoption of Agentic AI Services" on May 1 — the first coordinated multigovernment guidance specifically targeting agentic AI security. The guidance identifies five risk categories: privilege escalation, design and configuration flaws, behavioral unpredictability, structural vulnerabilities, and accountability gaps. Core recommendation: until evaluation methods and security standards mature, organizations should assume agentic AI systems may behave unexpectedly and prioritize resilience, reversibility, and risk containment over efficiency gains. Engineers deploying autonomous agents in production should treat this as required reading before granting agents access to sensitive systems or APIs.

**Source:** SecurityWeek | [Read more](https://www.securityweek.com/cisco-patches-another-sd-wan-zero-day-the-sixth-exploited-in-2026/)

Cisco disclosed CVE-2026-20182 on May 15 — a CVSS 10.0 authentication bypass in Catalyst SD-WAN Controller and SD-WAN Manager actively exploited by threat actor UAT-8616. The flaw targets the vdaemon service over DTLS (UDP port 12346), allowing unauthenticated remote attackers to inject an attacker-controlled public key into the vmanage-admin account's authorized SSH keys file, granting full administrative access without credentials. CISA added it to the KEV catalog with a mandatory 3-day federal remediation deadline; this is the sixth Cisco SD-WAN zero-day exploited in 2026, indicating sustained targeted interest in SD-WAN infrastructure. Administrators should patch immediately and audit SSH authorized_keys files for unauthorized entries.
