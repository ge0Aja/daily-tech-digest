### Cloudflare's Infire Engine Cuts LLM Inference Latency 3x

**Source:** Cloudflare Blog | [Read more](https://blog.cloudflare.com/high-performance-llms/)

Cloudflare published the architecture behind its Infire inference engine, which uses a disaggregated prefill/decode design — splitting compute-bound and memory-bound LLM processing stages onto dedicated hardware — to cut p90 inter-token latency from ~100ms to 20-30ms. The Rust-based engine achieves 20% higher throughput than vLLM baseline, and its Unweight compression method delivers 15–22% model weight reduction without accuracy loss. This is a detailed systems-design reference for engineers building or optimizing large-scale LLM serving infrastructure.

### Two Microsoft Defender Zero-Days Actively Exploited in the Wild

**Source:** The Hacker News | [Read more](https://thehackernews.com/2026/05/microsoft-warns-of-two-actively.html)

CISA added CVE-2026-41091 (CVSS 7.8) and CVE-2026-45498 (CVSS 4.0) to its Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog with a federal remediation deadline of June 3, 2026. CVE-2026-41091 exploits improper link resolution in Microsoft's Malware Protection Engine to escalate privileges to SYSTEM; CVE-2026-45498 can disable Defender entirely via denial-of-service. Patches are available in Antimalware Platform versions 1.1.26040.8 and 4.18.26040.7 — enterprise Windows teams should treat this as urgent.

## Industry & Trends

### CNN Sues Perplexity AI for Scraping 17,000 Stories Without a License

**Source:** CNN Business | [Read more](https://www.cnn.com/2026/05/28/media/cnn-sues-perplexity-ai-copyright)

CNN filed a copyright and trademark lawsuit against Perplexity on May 28, alleging the AI search company scraped 17,000+ stories, photos, and videos to power LLM responses without licensing or payment, and falsely advertised CNN premium content access via its "Comet Plus" tier despite no licensing agreement. The case — the first AI copyright suit by a television network — follows failed licensing negotiations last year and joins similar actions from The New York Times and Chicago Tribune. It signals an accelerating shift: AI companies that declined content licensing deals now face coordinated legal pressure from major publishers.

### xAI Sues Colorado Over AI Act; DOJ Intervenes to Block State Law

**Source:** Norton Rose Fulbright | [Read more](https://www.nortonrosefulbright.com/en-us/knowledge/publications/de3ad9de/xai-sues-doj-intervenes-enforcement-of-colorado-ai-act-suspended)

On April 9, Elon Musk's xAI sued to block Colorado's SB24-205 — the first comprehensive state AI law in the US, due June 30 — on First Amendment, Dormant Commerce Clause, and Due Process grounds. The DOJ intervened on April 24 to also oppose the law, marking the first federal intervention against a state AI statute. A judge stayed enforcement; Colorado responded by passing replacement legislation SB 26-189 (signed May 14), narrowing scope to automated decision-making systems rather than general AI.
