## Security & Privacy

### "Copy Fail" (CVE-2026-31431): Deterministic Local Root on All Major Linux Distros

**Source:** Wiz / Microsoft Security Blog | [Read more](https://www.wiz.io/blog/copyfail-cve-2026-31431-linux-privilege-escalation-vulnerability)

Disclosed April 29, Copy Fail is a CVSS 7.8 Linux kernel local privilege escalation affecting Ubuntu, Amazon Linux, RHEL, SUSE, Debian, Fedora, and Arch — any kernel shipped since 2017. The flaw exploits an in-place optimization in the AF_ALG socket interface via `splice()`, enabling an attacker to corrupt in-memory representations of privileged binaries (e.g., `/usr/bin/su`) without touching disk. The exploit is deterministic, race-condition-free, and fits in 732 bytes — making it especially dangerous in cloud, CI/CD, and Kubernetes environments where untrusted code runs. Patch to kernel commit `a664bf3d603d`; immediate mitigation is to blacklist the `algif_aead` module.

### Nx Console VS Code Extension Hijacked: Multi-Cloud Credential Harvester Targets 2.2M Installs

**Source:** The Hacker News | [Read more](https://thehackernews.com/2026/05/compromised-nx-console-18950-targeted.html)

On May 18, malicious version v18.95.0 of the Nx Console VS Code extension was published to the Marketplace for approximately 11 minutes, reaching thousands of developer machines out of its 2.2 million install base. The payload was a multi-stage credential stealer targeting GitHub Actions secrets, AWS/GCP/Azure/Kubernetes tokens, npm credentials, HashiCorp Vault, CircleCI — and notably `~/.claude/settings.json`, marking one of the first supply chain payloads explicitly targeting AI coding assistant credentials. The initial compromise traced to a TanStack supply chain attack that leaked a developer's GitHub credentials; threat group TeamPCP claimed responsibility, and approximately 3,800 internal GitHub repositories were exfiltrated.

### Microsoft Defender Zero-Days (CVE-2026-41091, CVE-2026-45498) Added to CISA KEV

**Source:** Help Net Security | [Read more](https://www.helpnetsecurity.com/2026/05/21/microsoft-defender-vulnerabilities-cve-2026-41091-cve-2026-45498/)

CISA added two actively exploited Microsoft Defender vulnerabilities to its Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog with a federal patch deadline of June 3, 2026. CVE-2026-41091 (CVSS 7.8) exploits improper link resolution in the Malware Protection Engine to escalate to SYSTEM; CVE-2026-45498 (CVSS 4.0) can disable Defender via denial-of-service. Patches are available in Antimalware Platform versions 1.1.26040.8 and 4.18.26040.7 — enterprise Windows teams should treat this as urgent given active exploitation.

## Industry & Trends

### Colorado AI Act Enters Enforcement June 30: The First Major US State AI Compliance Deadline

**Source:** VerifyWise | [Read more](https://verifywise.ai/blog/state-of-ai-governance-regulations-united-states-2026)

Colorado's AI Act — the most comprehensive state AI law in the US to date — enters enforcement on June 30 after a delayed start. It targets developers and deployers of "high-risk" AI systems making consequential decisions in education, employment, healthcare, housing, insurance, and legal services, requiring risk management programs, algorithmic bias mitigation, and consumer disclosures. With federal AI legislation stalled and the White House's National AI Policy Framework still at the recommendation stage, states remain the de facto regulatory layer; California's Transparency in Frontier AI Act adds penalties up to $1M per violation for frontier model developers with revenue over $500M.
