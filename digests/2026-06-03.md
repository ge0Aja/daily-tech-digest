## Security & Privacy

### Red Hat npm Supply Chain Attack: 32 Packages Backdoored with Credential-Stealing Worm

**Source:** Wiz Research | [Read more](https://www.wiz.io/blog/miasma-supply-chain-attack-targeting-redhat-npm-packages)

On June 1, Wiz Research found 96 versions across 32 `@redhat-cloud-services` npm packages backdoored with "Miasma," a worm linked to the TeamPCP Shai-Hulud supply chain campaign. Attackers compromised a Red Hat employee's GitHub account and injected payloads via GitHub Actions OIDC — not via a leaked npm token — into packages downloaded 116,991 times per week. The worm sweeps credentials across AWS, GCP, Azure, npm/PyPI publish tokens, SSH keys, Docker registries, GPG keys, and `.env` files, then self-propagates by republishing backdoored versions of other packages the victim account can access. Organizations using these packages should rotate all credentials immediately and audit CI/CD pipeline OIDC permissions.

### Palo Alto GlobalProtect CVE-2026-0257: Auth Bypass Under Active Exploitation, CISA KEV Deadline June 19

**Source:** Help Net Security | [Read more](https://www.helpnetsecurity.com/2026/06/01/hackers-are-exploiting-palo-alto-globalprotect-vpn-authentication-bypass-cve-2026-0257/)

CVE-2026-0257 is a critical authentication bypass in PAN-OS GlobalProtect that lets attackers forge authentication override cookies by extracting the public key from the server's TLS certificate chain — no credentials required. Rapid7 MDR confirmed two distinct attack waves starting May 18 and May 21, targeting local administrator accounts from infrastructure hosted at Vultr and Dromatics Systems. CISA added the flaw to its KEV catalog with a June 1 deadline for federal agencies and a June 19 deadline for broader remediation. Mitigation: disable the authentication override feature, or generate a dedicated certificate for it that is not exposed via HTTPS interfaces.

**Source:** Bleeping Computer | [Read more](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/google-fixes-one-actively-exploited-android-zero-day-124-flaws/)

Google's June 2026 Android security update patches 124 vulnerabilities, led by CVE-2025-48595 — an integer overflow in the Android Framework (CVSS 8.4) enabling privilege escalation without user interaction across Android 14, 15, 16, and 16-QPR2. ### Charter Communications (Spectrum) Breach: 13M Customer Records Leaked After Ransom Refusal

**Source:** Cybernews | [Read more](https://cybernews.com/security/charter-spectrum-data-breach-millions-exposed/)

ShinyHunters gained access to Charter's Salesforce CRM environment via a vishing attack against an employee's Microsoft Entra credentials, maintaining access for roughly two months starting April 1. After Charter refused to pay a ransom by a May 27 deadline, the group published 13M+ customer records including names, emails, home and company addresses, and support ticket contents; 27,000 employee records — including home addresses — were also exposed. Charter is the third major enterprise confirmed compromised via the same ShinyHunters/vishing/Salesforce vector in May 2026, following Cushman & Wakefield and 7-Eleven, suggesting a systematic targeting campaign.

### Kubernetes 2.0 Alpha: Native Multi-Cluster Control Plane and Unified Workload API

**Source:** Tech Insider | [Read more](https://tech-insider.org/kubernetes-2-0-everything-developers-need-to-know-about-the-biggest-release-in-a-decade/)

The first major version bump in Kubernetes's history targets alpha in H2 2026, with GA expected mid-2027. K8s 2.0 bakes multi-cluster awareness directly into the control plane — enabling placement policies, cross-cluster service discovery, and failover via native Kubernetes primitives, eliminating the need for third-party tools like KubeFed or Admiralty. A sweeping API consolidation merges Deployments, StatefulSets, and DaemonSets into a single resource type with declarative behavior modifiers, and permanently removes all previously deprecated API versions. Operators should begin auditing deprecated API usage now to plan upgrade timelines.

## Systems & Engineering

### Redis 8 Returns to Open Source: Tri-Licensed with AGPLv3

**Source:** Redis | [Read more](https://redis.io/blog/agplv3/)

Redis 8 is now available under three licenses: RSALv2, SSPLv1, and the OSI-approved AGPLv3 — marking Redis's return to open source a year after its controversial shift to SSPL, which triggered the successful Valkey fork. The change follows the November 2024 return of creator Salvatore Sanfillipo (antirez) to Redis Inc. Teams that avoided Redis due to SSPL concerns can now use Redis 8 under AGPLv3, though embedding it in proprietary software requires careful evaluation of AGPLv3's copyleft obligations. For teams that need a fully permissive license, Valkey remains the BSD-licensed alternative.

## Industry & Trends

### EU Cyber Resilience Act: Mandatory Vulnerability Reporting Kicks In September 11, 2026

**Source:** Hogan Lovells | [Read more](https://www.hoganlovells.com/en/publications/eu-cyber-resilience-act-getting-ready-for-cra-compliance-in-2026)

The EU CRA's vulnerability reporting obligations start September 11, 2026 — three months away and 15 months before full product compliance is required in December 2027. Any organization selling software products in the EU must report actively exploited vulnerabilities within 24 hours of becoming aware, issue full notifications within 72 hours, and file a final report within 14 days (or one month for severe incidents). Teams shipping to EU markets need to establish formal vulnerability disclosure pipelines and incident response workflows now; the CRA is the world's first horizontal cybersecurity regulation and carries significant penalties for non-compliance.
