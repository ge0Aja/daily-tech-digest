### Five Eyes Agencies Publish First-Ever Agentic AI Security Guidance
_CISA_ | [Read more](https://codilime.com/blog/notes-on-five-eyes-agentic-ai-guidance/)

On May 1, 2026, CISA, NSA, and their counterparts from Australia, Canada, New Zealand, and the UK jointly published "Careful Adoption of Agentic AI Services" — a 30-page document covering five risk categories: privilege escalation, design/configuration flaws, behavioral drift, structural risks, and supply-chain compromise. _BleepingComputer_ | [Read more](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/microsoft/microsoft-june-2026-patch-tuesday-fixes-6-zero-days-200-flaws/)

### Langflow CVE-2026-5027: Unauthenticated RCE Actively Exploited in the Wild
_The Hacker News_ | [Read more](https://thehackernews.com/2026/06/unpatched-langflow-flaw-cve-2026-5027.html)

CVE-2026-5027 (CVSS 8.8) is a path traversal flaw in Langflow's `POST /api/v2/files` endpoint that allows unauthenticated attackers to write arbitrary files to the server. Because Langflow enables auto-login out of the box, a single HTTP request yields a valid session token, enabling full RCE without credentials. VulnCheck added it to the Known Exploited Vulnerabilities list on June 8 after sensors detected in-the-wild attacks staging test files as a precursor to payload delivery. Upgrade to langflow-base 0.8.3 or Langflow 1.9.0 immediately on all self-hosted deployments.

### LangGraph Vulnerability Chain: SQLi to RCE in Popular AI Agent Framework
_Check Point Research_ | [Read more](https://research.checkpoint.com/2026/from-sqli-to-rce-exploiting-langgraphs-checkpointer/)

Check Point Research disclosed on June 12 a three-CVE chain in LangGraph (46.5M monthly downloads) that enables full server takeover: CVE-2025-67644 (SQL injection in the SQLite checkpointer), CVE-2026-28277 (unsafe msgpack deserialization), and CVE-2026-27022 (RediSearch query injection). The chain is exploitable on self-hosted deployments using SQLite or Redis checkpointers with user-controllable filter input, and exposes LLM API keys, customer data, and credentials wired to external systems. All three have been patched — upgrade langgraph-checkpoint-sqlite to 3.0.1, langgraph to 1.0.10, and @langchain/langgraph-checkpoint-redis to 1.0.1.

## Industry & Trends

### Great American AI Act of 2026: First Bipartisan Federal Framework Targets Frontier Models
_Captain Compliance_ | [Read more](https://captaincompliance.com/news/great-american-artificial-intelligence-act-of-2026-what-the-new-bipartisan-ai-bill-means-for-companies/)

Representatives Obernolte (R-CA) and Trahan (D-MA) released a 269-page discussion draft on June 4 — the most serious bipartisan federal AI legislation to date. The bill targets "frontier" AI systems and mandates safety, transparency, and accountability obligations for developers; directs NIST and NSF to fund AI education and workforce development; and calls on the Census Bureau and BLS to track AI adoption in federal surveys. With several competing bills in motion and state-level laws advancing in parallel, the federal preemption debate is intensifying.

### Colorado Rewrites Its AI Act: New Law Replaces June 30 Deadline With January 2027
_Ropes & Gray_ | [Read more](https://www.ropesgray.com/en/insights/alerts/2026/05/colorado-scales-back-ai-law-with-targeted-implications-for-health-care)

Colorado Governor Polis signed SB 26-189 on May 14, 2026, repealing and replacing the original Colorado AI Act (SB 24-205) before its June 30 enforcement date. The revised law narrows scope (with targeted implications for healthcare), pushes the compliance deadline to January 1, 2027, and requires the AG to provide a 60-day cure period before enforcement action. Developers and deployers who had prepared for the June 30 deadline now have more runway — but the AG retains the ability to skip the cure period for knowing or repeat violations.
