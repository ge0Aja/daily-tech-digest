### Agentjacking: Fake Sentry Errors Hijack AI Coding Agents via MCP Injection
**Source:** The Hacker News | [Read more](https://thehackernews.com/2026/06/agentjacking-attack-tricks-ai-coding.html)

Tenet Security disclosed "Agentjacking" — an attack that injects malicious instructions into Sentry error events using only a public, write-only DSN credential, causing AI coding agents (Claude Code, Cursor, Codex) to execute attacker-controlled shell commands on developer machines. The Sentry DSN is routinely exposed in browser JavaScript bundles and public GitHub repositories. In controlled testing, 100+ agents executed injected commands across 2,388 exposed organizations with no credentials required. The attack evades EDR, firewalls, and IAM entirely because nothing in the chain is unauthorized; Sentry acknowledged the issue but called it "technically not defensible" and deferred mitigation to model vendors — audit your Sentry DSN exposure and enforce agent sandboxing now.

### IBM OpenEvolve: LLM-Guided Framework Discovers 465 Quantum Error Correction Codes
**Source:** IBM Research | [Read more](https://research.ibm.com/blog/ai-for-qec)

IBM Research published OpenEvolve, an open-source, LLM-guided evolutionary framework for discovering quantum error correction (QEC) codes — a critical bottleneck in building fault-tolerant quantum computers. By using large language models to generate and evaluate algebraic expressions as QEC code candidates, the framework found 465 distinct viable codes, dramatically accelerating a search space that is computationally prohibitive with classical methods. IBM has fully open-sourced the library on GitHub, inviting the global quantum research community to extend and apply it across broader error correction domains.

## Security & Privacy

### Coupang Hit With Record $409M Fine After Breach Affecting 37.5M Users — 65% of South Korea
**Source:** The Record | [Read more](https://therecord.media/south-korea-data-breach-record-fine-coupang)

South Korea's Personal Information Protection Commission fined Coupang ₩624.7 billion ($409M) — a national record — after a data breach exposed the personal details of 37.55 million users, roughly 65% of the country's entire population. The breach was traced to a former employee who had developed Coupang's authentication system and retained active access credentials after departing in late 2024, a textbook insider-threat failure. The PIPC also found Coupang had collected 11.17 million users' third-party browsing activity without consent via its advertising platform. The case is a stark reminder that access revocation and least-privilege enforcement at offboarding are as critical as perimeter security.

**Source:** Hunton | [Read more](https://www.hunton.com/privacy-and-cybersecurity-law-blog/colorado-ai-act-amended-and-effective-date-delayed)

Colorado's landmark AI consumer protection regulation (SB 24-205) was effectively replaced after a federal magistrate stayed enforcement in April 2026 and the DOJ joined a constitutional challenge. Governor Polis signed the replacement bill (SB 26-189) on May 14, 2026, pushing the effective date from June 30, 2026 to January 1, 2027 and significantly scaling back obligations on AI developers and deployers. The revised law retains requirements around algorithmic discrimination, risk management, and impact assessments for high-risk AI systems, but reduces the burden on smaller developers. Organizations should continue governance program preparation — the deadline is delayed, not eliminated.
