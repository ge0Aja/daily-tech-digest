### Mollifier Layers: New Technique Makes Neural Networks Stable for Inverse PDE Problems
**Source:** ScienceDaily | [Read more](https://www.sciencedaily.com/news/computers_math/artificial_intelligence/)

Researchers at the University of Pennsylvania's School of Engineering introduced "Mollifier Layers," a technique that embeds classical mathematical smoothing functions directly into neural network architectures to solve inverse partial differential equations with significantly greater numerical stability and efficiency. The approach addresses a long-standing challenge in scientific AI where standard neural networks produce unstable solutions when working backward from observed data to underlying physical parameters. Applications span genomics, climate modeling, materials science, and chromatin biology; the work will be presented at NeurIPS 2026. This is part of a growing wave of physics-informed ML that could materially reduce simulation costs for computational engineering teams.

**Source:** Orca Security | [Read more](https://orca.security/resources/blog/cve-2026-20253-splunk-enterprise-rce-unauthenticated-file-operations/)

Splunk Enterprise's PostgreSQL Sidecar Service — introduced in version 10 — exposes unauthenticated HTTP backup and restore endpoints that are reachable through Splunk's main web proxy, allowing any network-reachable attacker to trigger arbitrary file operations and remote code execution with zero credentials (CVSS 9.8).
