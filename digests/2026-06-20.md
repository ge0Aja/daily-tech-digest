### Meta FAIR Releases CWM: 32B Open-Weights Code World Model Trained on Execution Traces
**Source:** AI at Meta | [Read more](https://ai.meta.com/research/publications/cwm-an-open-weights-llm-for-research-on-code-generation-with-world-models/)

Meta's Fundamental AI Research team released Code World Model (CWM), a 32-billion-parameter open-weights LLM mid-trained on Python interpreter execution traces and agentic Dockerized repository interactions — teaching the model to predict runtime execution states (stack frames, variable values, executed lines) rather than just tokens. CWM scores 65.8% on SWE-bench Verified (with test-time scaling), 68.6% on LiveCodeBench, and 96.6% on Math-500, while running on a single NVIDIA H100 (80 GB) with a 131K-token context window. Released under a non-commercial research license via Hugging Face, the world-model framing represents a meaningful architectural departure from standard next-token prediction for code, with implications for agent reliability and code understanding beyond generation.

### F5 Issues Out-of-Band Patches for Critical NGINX RCE Vulnerabilities (CVSS 9.2)
**Source:** Bleeping Computer / The Hacker News | [Read more](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/f5-issues-out-of-band-patches-for-critical-nginx-vulnerabilities/)

F5 released emergency out-of-band security patches on June 17 for two critical NGINX vulnerabilities scoring up to CVSS 9.2. CVE-2026-42530 is a use-after-free in the `ngx_http_v3_module` (HTTP/3 QUIC) enabling RCE or DoS on systems with ASLR disabled or bypassable; CVE-2026-42055 affects the `ngx_http_proxy_v2_module` and `ngx_http_grpc_module`, hitting NGINX Plus, Ingress Controller, Gateway Fabric, and App Protect WAF. Fixes are available in NGINX Plus R37.0.2.1/R36 P6 and NGINX Open Source 1.31.2/1.30.3; several WAF and DoS protection modules remain unpatched. No active exploitation has been observed, but NGINX's prevalence in Kubernetes ingress and API gateways makes this a high-priority patch for platform teams.

### OnlyFans "340M Record Leak" Turns Out to Be a Cross-Platform Compilation, Not a Direct Breach
**Source:** Security Affairs / TechRepublic | [Read more](https://securityaffairs.com/192643/cyber-crime/340-million-onlyfans-profiles-allegedly-rebuilt-from-leaks.html)

A threat actor listed 340 million purported OnlyFans user profiles for sale on a dark web forum (price: 0.313 BTC), but security researchers confirmed it is a data-stitching operation — profiles assembled by cross-referencing old Instagram, Twitter, and Spotify breach data against OnlyFans usernames — not a direct compromise of OnlyFans infrastructure. Both OnlyFans and the seller have denied any hack. Despite being a compilation rather than a fresh breach, the dataset still poses real harm: it correlates real-world identities with OnlyFans accounts, exposing creators and subscribers to targeted doxxing, harassment campaigns, and credential-stuffing attacks built around re-used email addresses.

**Source:** Holland & Knight / Latham & Watkins | [Read more](https://www.hklaw.com/en/insights/publications/2026/04/us-companies-face-eu-ai-acts-possible-august-2026-compliance-deadline)

EU lawmakers and member states reached a provisional agreement in May 2026 to overhaul the AI Act, pushing the enforcement deadline for high-risk AI system obligations (Articles 9–17, 26) from August 2, 2026 to December 2027 — a 16-month extension. The revision strips duplicated compliance requirements that created friction for manufacturers and broadens regulatory relief for SMEs; however, the maximum fine structure (€35M or 7% of global turnover) remains unchanged. High-risk categories include AI used in biometric identification, employment decisions, credit scoring, law enforcement, and healthcare — teams that had been sprinting toward August compliance now have additional runway, but the core requirements for risk management, data governance, logging, and human oversight remain mandatory at the new deadline.

### UK CMA Confirms Google and Apple Hold Strategic Market Status — Binding Remedies Expected
**Source:** Bloomberg Law | [Read more](https://news.bloomberglaw.com/bloomberg-law-analysis/analysis-2026-to-be-a-watershed-in-big-techs-antitrust-battles)

The UK's Competition and Markets Authority officially confirmed Google holds Strategic Market Status in general search and search advertising, while both Google and Apple hold SMS in mobile platforms. The decisions mark the UK as the most aggressive G7 jurisdiction on tech platform competition in 2026, and could force structural changes that ripple into how apps are distributed and how search defaults are negotiated globally.
