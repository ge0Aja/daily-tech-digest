## AI & Machine Learning

### Anthropic Fable 5 & Mythos 5 Suspended After US Export Control Order

**Source:** Al Jazeera / Axios | [Read more](https://www.axios.com/2026/06/16/ai-anthropic-export-controls)

On June 12, the Commerce Department's Bureau of Industry and Security ordered Anthropic to suspend access to its Fable 5 and Mythos 5 models for all foreign nationals, citing national security concerns. Because Anthropic couldn't reliably screen users by nationality, it disabled both models globally. The trigger was a China-linked group reportedly accessing the models and Amazon researchers identifying jailbreaks that could aid cyber exploitation. Anthropic is now suing the US administration after separately being blacklisted for refusing to allow the military to use its models for autonomous weapons systems.

**Source:** The Register / Tom's Hardware | [Read more](https://www.theregister.com/systems/2026/06/16/qualcomm-said-to-be-circling-ai-chip-biz-tenstorrent-in-10b-risc-v-power-play/5256084)

Qualcomm is in advanced negotiations to acquire Tenstorrent — Jim Keller's AI chip startup — at a valuation of $8–10 billion. This would be Qualcomm's second RISC-V acquisition this year, following the purchase of Ventana Micro Systems in December 2025. The deal would give Qualcomm a credible competitive position against Nvidia and AMD in AI hardware, though no final agreement has been reached.

### PyTorch 2.12 Ships with Major Distributed Systems and Compilation Updates

**Source:** PyTorch | [Read more](https://pytorch.org/event/pytorch-2-12-release-live-qa/)

Released May 13, 2026, PyTorch 2.12 delivers significant improvements across compilation, distributed systems, graph capture, export, and accelerator support. Key additions include a new device-agnostic `torch.accelerator.Graph` API, up to 100x faster batched eigenvalue decomposition on CUDA, and microscaling quantization format support in `torch.export.save`. The release expands platform support across CUDA, ROCm, XPU, MPS, and Arm — a high-impact update for teams running distributed ML training and inference at scale. Alibaba Cloud also joined the PyTorch Foundation as a Platinum Member.

## Security & Privacy

### ServiceNow Breach: Unauthenticated REST API Exposed Customer Instance Data

**Source:** Bleeping Computer | [Read more](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/servicenow-discloses-security-incident-exposing-customer-data/)

Attackers exploited an unauthenticated Scripted REST API endpoint (`/api/now/related_list_edit/create`) in ServiceNow between June 2–3, 2026, exposing IT tickets, embedded credentials, and employee records from enterprise customer instances. The root cause was a misconfiguration where `requires_authentication` was set to `false`. ServiceNow received a bug bounty report on April 22 but didn't apply a fix until June 5 — after active exploitation had already begun. Customers running the Australia platform release or older instances with certain configurations should check logs for activity from IP `51.159.98.241`.

### Verizon DBIR 2026: Vulnerability Exploitation Is Now the #1 Breach Vector

**Source:** SecurityWeek / Help Net Security | [Read more](https://www.helpnetsecurity.com/2026/05/20/verizon-2026-dbir-findings/)

The 2026 Verizon Data Breach Investigations Report marks a historic first: unpatched vulnerability exploitation has overtaken stolen credentials as the top initial access vector, accounting for roughly 31% of all breaches — the first time credential theft has been displaced in the report's 19-year history. The median time to fully patch vulnerabilities rose to 43 days (up from 32 in 2024), and organizations only patched 26% of CISA's Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog — down from 38% in 2024. AI-driven exploitation is compressing attacker timelines from months to hours, further pressuring already strained patch cycles.

### Google Patches 5th Chrome Zero-Day of 2026 (CVE-2026-11645)

**Source:** The Hacker News | [Read more](https://thehackernews.com/2026/06/chrome-v8-zero-day-cve-2026-11645.html)

Google patched its fifth actively exploited Chrome zero-day of 2026: CVE-2026-11645, an out-of-bounds read/write in the V8 JavaScript engine that enables remote code execution inside a sandbox via a crafted HTML page. Earlier 2026 zero-days included use-after-free bugs in Chrome's CSS and Dawn components and inappropriate V8 implementations (CVE-2026-2441, CVE-2026-3909, CVE-2026-3910, CVE-2026-5281). All Chrome users should update to version 149.0.7827.103 or later immediately.

### Google Launches Agent Sandbox as Native Kubernetes Primitive for AI Agents

**Source:** Google Cloud Blog | [Read more](https://cloud.google.com/blog/products/containers-kubernetes/agentic-ai-on-kubernetes-and-gke)

Announced at KubeCon NA 2025, Agent Sandbox is now rolling out as a native Kubernetes primitive designed for AI agent code execution and computer use workloads. ## Industry & Trends

### EU AI Act Code of Practice Finalized: Machine-Readable Watermarks Required by August 2

**Source:** European Commission / IPTC | [Read more](https://ec.europa.eu/commission/presscorner/detail/en/ip_26_1328)

The European Commission published the final Code of Practice on marking and labelling of AI-generated content on June 10, 2026, ahead of the August 2 enforcement date under Article 50 of the EU AI Act. Providers must embed machine-readable signals — watermarks, metadata, or content credentials — into AI-generated outputs. Deployers using generative AI for professional purposes must visually label AI content with standardized EU AI Office icons. The code is voluntary as a compliance mechanism, but the Article 50 obligations it supports are mandatory — making it the de facto standard for compliant AI content labeling in the EU.

### Colorado Hits Reset on AI Regulation: New Law Delays and Scales Back AI Act

**Source:** Morrison Foerster / Troutman Privacy | [Read more](https://www.mofo.com/resources/insights/260515-colorado-hits-reset-ai-regulation)

Colorado Governor Polis signed SB 189 on May 14, 2026, repealing and replacing the original Colorado AI Act (SB 24-205). The new law delays the effective date from June 30, 2026 to January 1, 2027, and significantly reduces compliance burden: it removes the duty-of-care framework targeting algorithmic discrimination, eliminates deployer requirements for risk management programs and impact assessments, and narrows the law's scope to transparency and disclosure. This rollback of the first U.S. state AI law may signal that comprehensive algorithmic accountability requirements face significant political resistance before they can take effect.
