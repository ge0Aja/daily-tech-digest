# Tech Digest — 2026-06-26

## AI & Machine Learning

### Google Gemini 2.5 Pro with Deep Think Is Now Rolling Out
_Google Blog_ | https://blog.google/products-and-platforms/products/gemini/gemini-2-5-deep-think/

Google launched Gemini 2.5 Pro with Deep Think on June 22, its most capable model to date. Deep Think uses parallel reasoning paths — decomposing problems, exploring alternatives, and verifying logic before responding — and leads benchmarks in science, math, and coding. The model features a 2 million token context window (entire codebases or full books in one session) and is rolling out to Google AI Ultra subscribers. This is Google's direct answer to OpenAI's o-series and Anthropic's extended thinking models.

### Qualcomm Eyes $10B Tenstorrent Acquisition, Confirms Modular Buy
_The Register_ | https://www.theregister.com/systems/2026/06/16/qualcomm-said-to-be-circling-ai-chip-biz-tenstorrent-in-10b-risc-v-power-play/5256084

Qualcomm is in advanced negotiations to acquire Tenstorrent — Jim Keller's RISC-V AI chip startup — for $8–10 billion, according to Reuters. Separately, at its June 24 Investor Day Qualcomm confirmed the acquisition of AI platform developer Modular in an all-stock deal worth ~$3.92B. For engineers, this could meaningfully improve inference portability outside the CUDA ecosystem.

## Security & Privacy

### Cisco Unified CM SSRF (CVE-2026-20230) Actively Exploited, Webshells Dropping
_BleepingComputer_ | https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/cisco-unified-cm-sme-flaw-cve-2026-20230-now-exploited-in-attacks/

A critical SSRF vulnerability (CVE-2026-20230, CVSS 8.6) in Cisco Unified Communications Manager is being actively exploited in the wild, with automated sweeps via Tor dropping JSP webshells. The attack chain abuses the WebDialer service to send crafted `file://` requests, deploy a rogue Apache Axis service, and write files that enable root escalation. WebDialer is disabled by default, so organizations that never enabled it are not exposed — but those who have it running should disable it immediately as a stopgap and apply Cisco's patch released June 3. Public PoC code is available, widening the attack surface rapidly.

### Klue Supply Chain Attack Exposes Salesforce Data at HackerOne, Huntress, Snyk, and Six Others
_TechCrunch_ | https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/22/klue-hack-results-in-data-breach-at-several-cybersecurity-firms/

Attackers used a compromised legacy service-account credential to push malicious code into Klue (a market intelligence SaaS), harvesting OAuth tokens that granted access to customers' Salesforce CRM environments. Nine organizations confirmed data exfiltration — HackerOne, Huntress, Jamf, Recorded Future, Tanium, Snyk, OneTrust, Sprout Social, and Insurity — with stolen data including contact info, pricing quotes, sales comms, and subscription details. The extortion group Icarus set a June 22 deadline for negotiations before publishing. The incident illustrates how a single compromised integration service account in a SaaS vendor can pivot into the CRM data of their entire customer base.

### French Government Messaging Platform Tchap Hacked; 13.5 GB Stolen
_SharkStriker_ | https://sharkstriker.com/blog/june-2026-data-breaches/

A threat actor breached Tchap, the French government's internal secure messaging platform, exfiltrating 73,467 user accounts (including personnel from multiple French Ministries), 643,459 messages, 876 chat rooms, and 59,386 shared media files — 13.5 GB in total. The breach is significant because Tchap was purpose-built to provide a secure WhatsApp alternative for government staff. The incident serves as a reminder that secure-by-design platforms still require robust access controls and regular audits; a single misconfigured endpoint or credential leak can compromise an otherwise well-architected system.

### Texas Parks and Wildlife: 3 Million Residents Potentially Exposed in License System Breach
_Kaseya_ | https://www.kaseya.com/blog/the-week-in-breach-news-06-24-26/

Texas Cyber Command detected a cybersecurity incident affecting the vendor behind the Texas Parks and Wildlife Department's hunting and fishing license system, potentially exposing personal data for more than three million Texans. The breach underscores an ongoing pattern: state and local government agencies with outsourced licensing or permit systems are high-value targets with comparatively low security maturity. Affected individuals should monitor for phishing attempts using the exposed contact information.

## Cloud & Infrastructure

### EU Launches Tech Sovereignty Package: Cloud and AI Development Act + Chips Act 2.0
_European Commission_ | https://commission.europa.eu/news-and-media/news/strengthening-europes-tech-sovereignty-2026-06-03_en

On June 3, the European Commission unveiled its most consequential digital policy package since GDPR: the Cloud and AI Development Act (CADA), Chips Act 2.0, an EU Open Source Strategy, and an AI energy roadmap. CADA aims to triple EU data center capacity by 2035, introduces a 4-level EU Cloud Sovereignty Framework (Level 1: data residency → Level 4: full supply-chain transparency), and adds procurement rules favouring locally assurance-certified services. For engineers at multinational companies, this will drive new compliance requirements around where cloud workloads run and what visibility governments have into the software stack — akin to what GDPR did for data handling.

_MindStudio_ | https://www.mindstudio.ai/blog/google-cloud-vs-aws-vs-azure-q1-2026-ai-infrastructure-race

## Industry & Trends

### EU Open Source Strategy: Mandate to Cut Dependency on US Big Tech
_CyberNews_ | https://cybernews.com/tech/europe-looks-for-ways-to-cut-cord-from-big-tech/

Alongside CADA, the EU's Open Source Strategy (part of the Tech Sovereignty Package) explicitly targets shifting government and critical infrastructure systems away from proprietary US-dominated software toward auditable open source alternatives. The strategy includes funding mechanisms for OSS projects deemed critical to EU infrastructure, analogous to the US CHIPS Act's investment in domestic semiconductor capacity. For open source maintainers and contributors, this represents a meaningful new source of institutional funding — and for companies with EU public-sector clients, a likely procurement shift toward open source stacks.

### NYC Schools Require AI Bias Review Before Any Tool Deployment
_MarketingProfs_ | https://www.marketingprofs.com/opinions/2026/54909/ai-update-june-5-2026-ai-news-and-views-from-the-past-week

New York City's Department of Education issued preliminary guidance requiring every AI tool to pass a bias and equity review before deployment across its schools. A comprehensive compliance playbook is scheduled for release later in June 2026. This is one of the first major US government bodies to operationalize AI procurement controls beyond privacy and data handling — and signals a direction that other large institutional buyers (healthcare, finance, other school districts) are likely to follow, adding a new compliance dimension for any AI product targeting the public sector.
