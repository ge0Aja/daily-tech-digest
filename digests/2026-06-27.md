## AI & Machine Learning

### MiniMax M3: Open-Weight Frontier Model with 1M-Token Context and Native Computer Use
_MiniMax_ | https://the-decoder.com/minimax-m3-open-weight-model-with-a-million-token-context-challenges-proprietary-leaders/

Released June 1, MiniMax M3 scores 59% on SWE-Bench Pro (ahead of GPT-5.5), 70% on OSWorld computer-use tasks, and 83.5 on BrowseComp. The model uses a novel MSA (MiniMax Sparse Attention) architecture supporting up to 1M-token contexts with 9× faster prefill and 15× faster decoding vs. its predecessor, and handles image/video input alongside native desktop computer operation. Weights and a full technical report are being open-sourced on Hugging Face and GitHub, making M3 the first credible open-weight alternative to closed frontier models for long-context agentic workflows.

### DeepSeek V3.2: 671B MoE, MIT Licensed, GPT-5 Class Performance
_DeepSeek_ | https://apxml.com/models/deepseek-v32

DeepSeek V3.2 is a 671B Mixture-of-Experts model that activates only 37B parameters per token, delivering GPT-5-class performance at a fraction of inference cost. It introduces DeepSeek Sparse Attention (DSA) for efficient long-context handling and achieved gold-medal results on the 2025 IMO and IOI benchmarks. Released under the MIT license, it enables fully self-hosted commercial deployments — currently the strongest open-weight option for organizations needing frontier math and coding capabilities without proprietary API lock-in.

_European Commission_ | https://ec.europa.eu/commission/presscorner/detail/en/ip_26_1187

The EU is finalizing its Code of Practice for marking and labeling AI-generated content under AI Act transparency obligations, with a first draft published December 2025 and finalization expected this month. ## Security & Privacy

### FortiBleed: 86,000+ FortiGate Firewalls Compromised Across 194 Countries
_Arctic Wolf / CISA_ | https://arcticwolf.com/resources/blog/active-fortibleed-campaign-impacting-fortinet-devices-across-194-countries/

A fully automated credential-harvesting campaign dubbed FortiBleed has compromised roughly 86,644 internet-facing Fortinet FortiGate devices — approximately 50% of all reachable Fortinet firewalls — across 194 countries. Attackers scan for vulnerable devices, brute-force known passwords, harvest VPN credentials from compromised firewalls in transit, and crack stored SHA-256 hashes offline. There is no patch; CISA has issued an urgent advisory to rotate all VPN and admin credentials immediately, enforce MFA on every admin and VPN account, and remove management interfaces from public internet exposure.

### Microsoft June 2026 Patch Tuesday: Record 206 Vulnerabilities Including HTTP/2 Bomb Zero-Day
_Malwarebytes_ | https://www.malwarebytes.com/blog/bugs/2026/06/microsofts-biggest-ever-patch-tuesday-fixes-206-bugs-including-3-zero-days

Microsoft's June 2026 Patch Tuesday is the largest in the program's 23-year history, fixing 206 flaws including 39 Critical and 3 publicly disclosed zero-days. The most critical is CVE-2026-49160, a Denial of Service flaw in Windows HTTP.sys tied to the HTTP/2 Bomb amplification technique. Also addressed: a BitLocker bypass via physical access (CVE-2026-50507) and a CTFMON vulnerability (CVE-2026-45586). Microsoft attributed the record patch volume in part to increased use of AI-assisted vulnerability discovery across its products.

### ShinyHunters Exploits Oracle PeopleSoft Zero-Day to Breach 100+ Organizations
_The Hacker News_ | https://thehackernews.com/2026/06/shinyhunters-exploits-oracle-peoplesoft.html

Between May 27 and June 9, ShinyHunters exploited CVE-2026-35273, a critical unauthenticated RCE zero-day in Oracle PeopleTools 8.61/8.62, breaching more than 100 organizations before Oracle published an out-of-band patch on June 10. 68% of victims are U.S. higher education institutions; the University of Nottingham confirmed ~455,000 student records leaked including passport numbers and disability details. Attackers deployed MeshCentral agents disguised as cloud endpoints for persistence and are actively extorting victims — organizations running PeopleSoft should patch immediately and hunt for MeshCentral indicators of compromise.

### Tchap: France's Mandatory Government Messaging Platform Breached via Social Engineering
_SecurityWeek_ | https://www.securityweek.com/french-government-messaging-platform-breached-by-mysterious-misere-hacker/

France's ANSSI detected a breach of Tchap — the government's mandatory Matrix-based secure messaging platform for all civil servants — on June 7, after an attacker socially engineered a valid education-sector account. The hacker claims 73,467 user accounts, 643,459 messages, and 13.5 GB of data including files labeled "Diffusion Restreinte." Officials note end-to-end encrypted private conversations were not exposed, but public chat rooms were accessible through the hijacked account. The incident highlights the targeting risk created when governments mandate a single platform for all internal communications.

## Cloud & Infrastructure

### Kubernetes v1.36: User Namespaces GA, DRA Maturation, AI Workload Scheduling
_Kubernetes.io_ | https://kubernetes.io/blog/2026/04/22/kubernetes-v1-36-release/

Kubernetes v1.36 ships 70 enhancements with 18 reaching Stable. Key graduations include User Namespaces GA (mapping container root to an unprivileged host user, eliminating a major container escape vector), MutatingAdmissionPolicy GA (native mutation logic without external webhook services or TLS juggling), and Fine-Grained Kubelet API Authorization. Dynamic Resource Allocation (DRA) matures with support for GPU/memory/CPU resources, enabling richer AI/ML workload scheduling. New alpha: Workload-Aware Scheduling (WAS) with gang scheduling for co-dependent pod groups.

### EU Proposes Cloud and AI Development Act: Four-Level Sovereignty Framework
_European Commission_ | https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/cloud-and-ai-development-act

On June 3, the European Commission proposed the Cloud and AI Development Act (CADA) to address EU dependency on non-EU hyperscalers and a structural datacenter capacity deficit. The regulation defines four cloud sovereignty assurance levels — from basic EU entity requirements to full supply chain transparency and zero third-country interference — which public sector bodies must select based on risk assessments. The act aims to triple EU datacenter capacity by 2035 and will impose significant new compliance obligations on AWS, Azure, and GCP serving European public sector customers.

## Industry & Trends

### Colorado Revises Its AI Law — Original Act Replaced, Effective Date Pushed to Jan 1, 2027
_Troutman Pepper_ | https://www.troutmanprivacy.com/2026/05/colorado-legislature-passes-bill-to-repeal-and-replace-colorado-ai-act/

Governor Polis signed SB 189 on May 14, replacing the original Colorado AI Act (SB24-205) and pushing the effective date from June 30, 2026 to January 1, 2027. The replacement eliminates the duty-of-care standard against algorithmic discrimination, mandatory risk management programs for deployers, and Attorney General reporting requirements from the original law. Teams that were racing for the June 30 deadline now have additional runway, but should note the January 2027 date is firm and the revised law still carries significant compliance obligations for high-risk AI system developers and deployers.

### 900M Lines of Linux System Source Code Permanently Archived in Software Heritage
_Open Invention Network_ | https://openinventionnetwork.com/june-2026-insider/

The Open Invention Network permanently archived the OIN 2.0 Linux System — 5,100+ packages and 900 million lines of source code — in the Software Heritage repository, the world's largest public software archive. The move protects the open-source Linux ecosystem from link rot and hosting failures, ensuring long-term access to the foundational packages underlying virtually all cloud and embedded infrastructure. For teams managing software supply chain compliance or open-source license auditing, this guarantees canonical source availability for the packages forming the Linux ecosystem baseline.
