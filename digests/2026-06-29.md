## AI & Machine Learning

### Five Eyes Issue First-Ever Agentic AI Security Guidance
_The Register_ | https://www.theregister.com/2026/05/04/five_eyes_agentic_ai_recommendations/

Six national cybersecurity agencies — CISA, NSA, and the UK, Australian, Canadian, and New Zealand NCSSCs — jointly published "Careful Adoption of Agentic AI Services," a 30-page document cataloguing 5 risk categories, 23 specific risks, and over 100 best practices for deploying AI agents in production environments. The document identifies privilege abuse, supply-chain compromise, and behavioral unpredictability as the primary threat classes, and recommends cryptographically secured agent identities, short-lived credentials, and mandatory human approval gates for high-impact actions such as financial transactions, regulated record updates, or irreversible system changes. This is the first coordinated multinational policy document specifically addressing AI agent security — signalling that agentic AI attack surfaces have been elevated from research concern to national security imperative, and that voluntary guidance here is likely a precursor to regulatory requirements.

## Security & Privacy

### ShinyHunters Exploits Oracle PeopleSoft Zero-Day CVE-2026-35273 Across 100+ Organizations
_The Hacker News_ | https://thehackernews.com/2026/06/shinyhunters-exploits-oracle-peoplesoft.html

ShinyHunters (tracked by Google Mandiant as UNC6240) exploited CVE-2026-35273 — a CVSS 9.8 unauthenticated RCE flaw in Oracle PeopleSoft Enterprise PeopleTools, requiring only HTTP network access with no login or user interaction — against approximately 300 PeopleSoft instances between May 27 and June 9, before Oracle published an out-of-band advisory on June 10, making this a zero-day the entire time. Over 100 organizations were confirmed compromised, 68% in higher education; Have I Been Pwned counted approximately 455,000 unique email addresses in leaked data sets covering names, addresses, phone numbers, passport numbers, and disability details for current students and alumni. The attack script (`[victim]_fanout.sh`) spreads laterally over SSH using credentials harvested from internal hosts — organizations running PeopleSoft should patch immediately and audit SSH access logs from late May onward.

### Icarus/Klue Supply Chain Attack: OAuth Token Harvesting Exfiltrates Salesforce CRM Data from Major Security Vendors
_Cybersecurity Dive_ | https://www.cybersecuritydive.com/news/klue-investigating-supply-chain-attack-salesforce-integrations/823532/

A newly identified criminal gang, Icarus, gained access to Klue's integration infrastructure on June 11–12 via a compromised legacy service-account credential, then pushed a malicious code update to harvest OAuth tokens used to authorize Klue's connections with customer SaaS platforms — primarily Salesforce. Using the stolen tokens, attackers executed nearly 1,000 Salesforce REST API queries in 15 minutes during peak activity with sustained exfiltration windows over 6 hours; confirmed victims include Huntress, Recorded Future, Snyk, Sprout Social, Tanium, and Gong, with contact data, pricing information, sales communications, and internal notes exfiltrated. This is a canonical SaaS supply-chain attack: a single compromised vendor's service account became a pass-through into at least six downstream production environments — the lesson is that every third-party OAuth integration is a lateral movement vector, not just a feature dependency.

### Tchap French Government Messaging Platform Hacked: 73K Ministry Accounts and 643K Messages Exposed
_SharkStriker_ | https://sharkstriker.com/blog/june-2026-data-breaches/

Attackers compromised Tchap — the self-hosted Matrix-based messaging platform used by French government ministries — stealing 13.5 GB of data including 73,467 user accounts from French Ministries, 643,459 messages, 876 chat rooms, and 59,386 shared media files. Tchap was introduced specifically to give the French government a sovereign, auditable alternative to commercial messaging platforms; the breach demonstrates that self-hosted deployments do not automatically confer security advantages without the same rigorous access control, patching cadence, and incident response posture required by any production system. For engineers operating federated or self-hosted messaging infrastructure, this is a concrete reminder that server-side hardening — not transport encryption — is the dominant breach surface.

### EU Cyber Resilience Act: Mandatory Vulnerability Reporting Obligations Start September 11, 2026
_HeroDevs_ | https://www.herodevs.com/blog-posts/cra-reporting-obligations-start-september-2026-what-eol-dependencies-mean-for-your-compliance

Starting September 11, 2026, the EU Cyber Resilience Act requires manufacturers of products with digital elements to report actively exploited vulnerabilities to ENISA and national CSIRTs within 24 hours of becoming aware of them — the first major compliance deadline ahead of full CRA enforcement in December 2027. Liability attaches to whoever holds the commercial relationship with a European entity: distributors, importers, and manufacturers bundling open-source components are all in scope, while unpaid maintainers with no commercial relationship are explicitly exempt under the Act's open-source carve-out. Teams shipping software products into European markets should audit for end-of-life dependencies now — an exploited EOL component post-September triggers a 24-hour mandatory disclosure event.

### Apache Software Foundation Launches Responsible AI Initiative
_Open Source Initiative_ | https://opensource.org/blog/the-2026-state-of-open-source-report

The Apache Software Foundation launched its Responsible AI Initiative on April 10, 2026, establishing guidelines for ASF-umbrella projects that emphasize human oversight requirements, licensing integrity, security reviews, and documentation standards for AI components.
