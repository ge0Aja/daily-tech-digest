_MIT News_ | https://news.mit.edu/2026/improving-ai-agent-speed-and-energy-efficiency-0625

Researchers from MIT and Microsoft developed Murakkab, an intelligent system that automates the design and deployment of multi-model agentic workflows. ## Security & Privacy

### "Bad Epoll" CVE-2026-46242: Unprivileged Root Exploit in Linux 6.4+ Hits Servers and Android
_The Hacker News / TechTimes_ | https://thehackernews.com/2026/07/new-bad-epoll-linux-kernel-flaw-lets.html

CVE-2026-46242, dubbed "Bad Epoll," is a use-after-free race condition in the Linux kernel's epoll subsystem: when two epoll file descriptors monitoring each other are closed simultaneously, the kernel reads and writes through a freed pointer, enabling arbitrary memory corruption and privilege escalation to root. Researcher Jaeyoung Chung discovered the bug via a kernelCTF ($71,337+ bounty) submission and published a 99% reliable exploit publicly. The flaw affects kernels v6.4 and newer, impacting Linux servers, desktops, and Android devices (Pixel 8 running v6.1 is unaffected); the bug evades KASAN, the kernel's primary memory safety detector. A fix is available — distributions shipping affected kernels should patch immediately, and security teams should treat public exploit availability as urgent.

### Avalon Modular Malware Framework Bundles CrownX Ransomware — AI-Assisted, Zero AV Detections Since March
_The Hacker News / Rescana_ | https://thehackernews.com/2026/07/new-avalon-malware-framework-packs.html

Researchers discovered Avalon, a previously undocumented modular malware framework distributed via multi-stage phishing: targets receive a spoofed legal document email linking to a password-protected archive on Proton Drive, with the payload embedded in an ISO image to bypass email-layer scanning. Once executed, Avalon harvests credentials and cookies from Chromium and Firefox, cryptocurrency wallet data (MetaMask, Phantom, Exodus, Ledger Live), Slack, Teams, Discord, and Windows Credential Manager, before deploying the CrownX ransomware component that specifically targets backup and recovery systems to maximize leverage. The artifact was uploaded to VirusTotal in March 2026 and still has zero detections across all engines. Code analysis indicates AI-assisted development, consistent with the broader trend of adversaries using LLMs to accelerate malware construction with minimal manual coding effort.

## Systems & Engineering

### PostgreSQL 18 Released: New Async I/O Subsystem, 3× Read Performance, OAuth 2.0, Skip-Scan B-Tree
_PostgreSQL.org_ | https://www.postgresql.org/about/news/postgresql-18-released-3142/

PostgreSQL 18 ships a rewritten asynchronous I/O subsystem delivering up to 3× performance improvement on storage reads — the most significant performance architecture change in years, benefiting read-heavy OLTP and analytics workloads alike. New query optimization features include skip-scan on multicolumn B-tree indexes (accelerating queries that omit a leading index column equality condition) and OR condition index optimization. Developer-facing additions include a uuidv7() function for monotonic sortable UUIDs with better index locality, virtual generated columns that compute at query time rather than storing values, and OAuth 2.0 SSO integration for enterprise identity providers. The pg_upgrade utility gains a `--swap` flag that swaps data directories instead of copying files, dramatically reducing upgrade downtime on large instances; planner statistics are now preserved across major version upgrades, eliminating post-upgrade performance regressions during the statistics warm-up period.

## Industry & Trends

### EU AI Act Omnibus Approved: High-Risk AI Deadlines Deferred from August 2026 to December 2027
_Gibson Dunn / Travers Smith_ | https://www.gibsondunn.com/eu-ai-act-omnibus-agreement-postponed-high-risk-deadlines-and-other-key-changes/

The EU Council gave final approval on June 29 to the AI Act Omnibus — a simplification package that shifts the compliance deadline for high-risk AI systems listed in Annex III (recruitment tools, credit scoring, law enforcement, education, border control) from August 2, 2026, to December 2, 2027; systems embedded in regulated products under Annex I (medical devices, machinery, vehicles) move to August 2028. The amendment also reinstates the obligation for providers to register AI systems in the EU high-risk database — including systems claiming an exemption from high-risk classification — and shortens the watermarking deadline for AI-generated content from six months to three, with compliance due December 2026. Teams that had been preparing August 2026 compliance programs get a 16-month runway extension; teams shipping AI systems that generate synthetic content face an earlier watermarking obligation than previously expected.

_Built In / Interesting Engineering_ | https://builtin.com/articles/tech-laws-2026

A wave of US state-level tech legislation is going into effect across 2026, covering three primary domains: AI transparency obligations (disclosure when AI is used in consequential decisions), data privacy rights (access, deletion, and portability requirements modeled on GDPR), and children's online safety (age verification and content restrictions). Engineering teams shipping consumer-facing applications or HR/lending software in the US should audit their data flows and decision automation against each state's law; legal aggregator tools that track state-level compliance requirements are increasingly necessary for US tech companies.
