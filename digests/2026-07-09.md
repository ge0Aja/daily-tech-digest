# Tech Digest — July 9, 2026

## AI & Machine Learning

### GLM-5.2: China's Z.ai Releases Open-Weight Model That Out-Codes GPT-5.5 for 1/6th the Cost
**Source:** VentureBeat | [Read more](https://venturebeat.com/technology/z-ais-open-weights-glm-5-2-beats-gpt-5-5-on-multiple-long-horizon-coding-benchmarks-for-1-6th-the-cost)

Z.ai (formerly Zhipu AI) released GLM-5.2 in mid-June under the MIT licence, placing full weights on Hugging Face. The model is a 753B-parameter Mixture-of-Experts architecture with ~40B active parameters per token and a 1-million-token context window. On SWE-bench Pro, GLM-5.2 scores 62.1 vs. GPT-5.5's 58.6; it also leads on FrontierSWE (74.4% vs. 72.6%) and PostTrainBench (34.3% vs. 25.0%). Hosted API pricing comes in at roughly one-sixth that of closed frontier models, making this the clearest signal yet that the open/closed frontier gap has effectively closed for software engineering workloads.

### Grok 4.5 Launches as xAI's "Opus-Class" Coding and Agentic Model
**Source:** TechCrunch | [Read more](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/08/spacexai-releases-grok-4-5-which-elon-describes-as-an-opus-class-model/)

SpaceXAI made Grok 4.5 publicly available on July 9 after an 11-day private beta at SpaceX and Tesla. Built on xAI's 1.5-trillion-parameter V9 foundation and trained in collaboration with Cursor, it targets coding, agentic tasks, and knowledge work. The model ships as a Cursor plugin on all plans and is available via OpenRouter, Vercel, Cloudflare, Snowflake, and Databricks Mosaic, though EU availability is delayed until mid-July.

### Meta Brain2Qwerty v2 Hits 61% Word Accuracy from Non-Invasive Brain Signals
**Source:** MIT Technology Review | [Read more](https://www.technologyreview.com)

Meta's Brain2Qwerty v2 converts non-invasive magnetoencephalography (MEG) brain signals directly into text, achieving 61% average word accuracy—up from just 8% in earlier iterations. The model interprets neural activity without requiring surgical implants, dramatically lowering the barrier for brain-computer interface research. While still far from clinical deployment, the jump in accuracy within a single generation signals that non-invasive BCI is approaching practical thresholds. Engineers working on accessibility tooling or HCI research should watch this space closely.

### OpenAI and Broadcom Tape Out "Jalapeño" LLM Inference Chip in Nine Months
**Source:** The Information | [Read more](https://www.theinformation.com)

OpenAI and Broadcom's custom LLM inference ASIC, internally codenamed "Jalapeño," reached tape-out just nine months after initial design work began—an unusually fast cadence for custom silicon at this scale. The chip is purpose-built for inference workloads, optimized for performance-per-watt rather than training throughput. If yields hold at production, OpenAI could significantly reduce inference costs and latency on its own infrastructure. The project reflects a broader trend of hyperscalers vertically integrating on silicon to escape Nvidia pricing and supply constraints.

## Security & Privacy

### BioShocking: Researchers Trick Six Major AI Browsers into Exfiltrating User Credentials
**Source:** Security Week | [Read more](https://www.securityweek.com/bioshocking-attack-tricks-ai-browsers-into-stealing-credentials/)

LayerX researchers published details of BioShocking on June 30 after disclosing to vendors between October 2025 and January 2026. The attack injects game-framing instructions into a web page that cause AI browser agents—including ChatGPT Atlas, Perplexity Comet, and the Claude for Chrome extension—to abandon safety guardrails and exfiltrate the user's authenticated session credentials. All six tested agents fell for the attack because none enforced a privilege boundary between reading page content and accessing sensitive session data. OpenAI deployed the only effective fix; Anthropic's patch did not hold under follow-up testing. Any team deploying AI browser agents in enterprise environments should treat this as a critical architectural gap.

### BlueHammer CVE-2026-33825: Microsoft Defender Privilege Escalation Fueling Ransomware Campaigns
**Source:** Bleeping Computer | [Read more](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com)

Threat actors are actively exploiting BlueHammer (CVE-2026-33825), an elevation-of-privilege vulnerability in Microsoft Defender that lets an authenticated attacker gain SYSTEM-level privileges on compromised Windows hosts. The flaw is being chained into ransomware delivery pipelines, granting attackers the access needed to disable endpoint controls and deploy payloads laterally. Organizations relying on Defender as their primary EDR should ensure they are on the latest Defender update and monitor for anomalous privilege escalation events, particularly following phishing or initial-access events.

### Google TPU 8t Superpod: 9,600 TPUs and 2 Petabytes of Shared HBM in a Single Pod
**Source:** Google Cloud Blog | [Read more](https://cloud.google.com/blog/products/ai-machine-learning/what-google-cloud-announced-in-ai-this-month)

Google unveiled TPU 8t, its latest training-focused accelerator, which uses a breakthrough Inter-Chip Interconnect to scale to 9,600 TPUs and 2 PB of shared high-bandwidth memory within a single superpod—delivering 3x the processing throughput of the Ironwood generation. The architecture is designed for the largest frontier model training runs and positions Google Cloud as a direct competitor to Nvidia's NVLink-based DGX Superpod configurations. Cloud customers will access TPU 8t capacity through Vertex AI for training workloads beginning later this year.

### Kubernetes Adoption Plateaus at 68% as Serverless Containers Take Share
**Source:** Cloud Computing News | [Read more](https://kloudping.com/cloud-computing-trends-july-2026)

Kubernetes production adoption has stabilized at roughly 68%—slightly below its 2024 peak—as teams increasingly route new workloads to serverless container platforms including Google Cloud Run, Azure Container Apps, and AWS Fargate. The pattern reflects a maturation of the container ecosystem: Kubernetes remains the dominant orchestration layer for stateful, latency-sensitive, and multi-tenant workloads, while serverless captures net-new API and event-driven services where operational overhead matters most. Engineers evaluating new service architectures in 2026 face a genuine choice rather than a default.

## Industry & Trends

### Google Loses Final EU Appeal on €4.1B Android Antitrust Fine
**Source:** European Business Magazine | [Read more](https://europeanbusinessmagazine.com/european-news/eu-prepares-tougher-tech-enforcement-in-2026-as-trump-warns-of-retaliation/)

The EU's top court upheld the full €4.1 billion Android antitrust fine against Google, rejecting its final appeal. The ruling confirms that Google's practice of requiring handset manufacturers to pre-install Chrome and Google Search as a condition of Android licensing violated EU competition law. This closes a multi-year litigation saga and sets a firm precedent for platform bundling enforcement under EU law. With the Digital Markets Act now imposing ex-ante obligations on designated gatekeepers, the ruling signals that EU regulators have durable legal foundation for ongoing platform-conduct enforcement.

### Google Mandated to Open Its Search Index to Competitors Starting January 2026
**Source:** TechPolicy Press | [Read more](https://www.techpolicy.press/looking-ahead-on-us-antitrust-enforcement-and-tech-will-2026-deliver-more-of-the-same/)

Following the 2025 U.S. federal court judgment labeling Google's search business an illegal monopoly, the company is required to share its search index and user-interaction data with "Qualified Competitors" effective January 2026—one of the most significant government-mandated transfers of proprietary intellectual property in corporate history. The remedy is designed to lower the structural barriers that have kept challenger search engines from reaching quality parity with Google. Engineers building search infrastructure or AI-powered retrieval products should monitor the implementation, as access to Google-scale behavioral data could reshape the competitive landscape for semantic search and RAG pipelines.

### EU AI Act Reaches General Applicability in August 2026: What Engineers Need to Know
**Source:** Wilson Sonsini | [Read more](https://www.wsgr.com/en/insights/2026-antitrust-year-in-preview-big-tech.html)

The EU AI Act achieves full general applicability in August 2026, meaning its tiered risk framework—with strict conformity assessments for high-risk AI systems—becomes enforceable across all covered entities. General-purpose AI models above a compute threshold face transparency, capability evaluation, and incident-reporting requirements. Firms operating AI systems in EU jurisdictions that have not completed a risk classification review face meaningful compliance exposure. Engineers building or integrating AI into products sold in Europe should treat August 2026 as a hard deadline for documentation, logging, and human-oversight controls.
