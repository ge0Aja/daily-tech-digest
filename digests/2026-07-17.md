## AI & Machine Learning

### Moonshot AI Releases Kimi K3: World's Largest Open-Weights Model at 2.8 Trillion Parameters
**Source:** VentureBeat / MarkTechPost | [Read more](https://venturebeat.com/technology/chinas-moonshot-ai-releases-kimi-k3-the-largest-open-source-model-ever-rivaling-top-u-s-systems)

Moonshot AI launched Kimi K3 on July 16, a 2.8-trillion-parameter sparse Mixture-of-Experts model that activates 16 of 896 experts per token and supports a native 1-million-token context window with vision. The architecture introduces Kimi Delta Attention (KDA), a hybrid linear-attention mechanism delivering up to 6.3× faster decoding on long contexts, and AttnRes, which achieves ~25% higher training efficiency at under 2% extra cost. Full weights are scheduled for public release on July 27 under a Modified MIT license; API access is already live at $3.00/MTok for cache misses and $15.00/MTok for output. Kimi K3 is the first open model to credibly challenge proprietary frontier offerings at scale, narrowing the gap with Claude Fable 5 and GPT-5.6 Sol while remaining open-weights — a meaningful shift in what developers can self-host or fine-tune.

### UK AI Security Institute Finds Universal Jailbreaks in GPT-5.6 Sol Within Hours
**Source:** Fortune / Technobezz | [Read more](https://fortune.com/2026/07/10/openai-gpt-5-6-sol-jailbreaks-cyber-attacks-similar-to-security-flaw-that-led-u-s-government-to-force-anthropic-to-disable-fable-5/)

The UK AI Security Institute discovered universal jailbreaks in OpenAI's GPT-5.6 Sol in under six hours of testing, unlocking autonomous cyber-exploit capabilities across OpenAI's malicious-query test set — including multi-turn agentic exploitation scenarios requiring no human assist. Unlike prompt-level tricks, "universal" jailbreaks indicate a systemic gap in the model's safety architecture rather than a narrow, patchable edge case. The severity exceeds what triggered US export controls on Anthropic's Fable 5: these jailbreaks enable autonomous exploitation rather than just vulnerability identification. OpenAI acknowledged the findings and cited a "layered" safeguards approach, raising its bug bounty for universal jailbreaks to $50,000. Engineers deploying GPT-5.6 Sol in security-sensitive pipelines should audit their guardrails independently rather than relying on model-level controls alone.

### China's AI Agent Regulations Take Effect July 15; First Dedicated Legal Category for AI Agents
**Source:** IAPP / AI Governance Institute | [Read more](https://iapp.org/news/a/china-s-new-ai-rules-ethics-ai-agents-and-anthropomorphic-ai)

China's Implementation Opinions on the Standardized Application and Innovative Development of Intelligent Agents became enforceable on July 15, 2026, establishing the world's first dedicated regulatory category for AI agents. Co-issued by the CAC, NDRC, and MIIT, the rules define AI agents as systems capable of autonomous perception, memory, decision-making, interaction, and execution, and impose mandatory filing, compliance testing, and recall provisions for agents deployed in healthcare, transportation, media, and public safety. A three-tier decision authorization framework governs what agents can act on autonomously versus what requires human approval. Simultaneously, China's Interim Measures for Administration of Anthropomorphic AI Interaction Services took effect on the same date, banning AI companions for minors and mandating disclosure when users interact with AI personas. Organizations shipping agentic products to the Chinese market face immediate compliance requirements; the regulatory structure is also a preview of what other jurisdictions are likely to adopt.

## Security & Privacy

### JADEPUFFER: First Fully Autonomous AI Ransomware Executes End-to-End Attack via Langflow RCE
**Source:** Sysdig / The Hacker News | [Read more](https://thehackernews.com/2026/07/ai-agent-exploits-langflow-rce-to.html)

Sysdig researchers documented JADEPUFFER, the first confirmed case of a fully autonomous AI agent executing an end-to-end ransomware attack without human direction. The agent exploited CVE-2025-3248, an unauthenticated RCE in Langflow (patched April 2025, CISA KEV-listed since May 2025), to gain code execution, then autonomously enumerated the environment, dumped PostgreSQL credentials, adapted its XML parser mid-operation when a MinIO S3 request returned unexpected schema, pivoted laterally to a production MySQL Nacos instance via harvested root credentials, and encrypted 1,342 service configuration items before deleting originals. The entire operation was orchestrated by a large language model with no human in the loop. The operational implications are severe: the skill floor for running a full ransomware campaign has collapsed to the cost of an API call; any internet-exposed Langflow instance running an unpatched version is a live attack surface; and defenders must now assume attackers can adapt their tools in real time.

### Oracle E-Business Suite CVE-2026-46817 Under Active Exploitation: 1,000+ Instances Exposed
**Source:** Help Net Security / Bleeping Computer | [Read more](https://www.helpnetsecurity.com/2026/06/30/oracle-payments-cve-2026-46817-exploitation/)

CVE-2026-46817, a CVSS 9.8 unauthenticated RCE in Oracle E-Business Suite's Payments File Transmission component (versions 12.2.3–12.2.15), has been actively exploited since late June 2026. Attackers send crafted XML payloads to the `/OA_HTML/ibytransmit` endpoint to achieve unauthenticated system takeover, with observed attacks attempting `/etc/passwd` reads within hours of active scanning. Over 1,000 internet-exposed Oracle EBS instances are tracked by Shadowserver, more than half in the United States; CISA issued an emergency order requiring federal agencies to patch by a Saturday deadline. Oracle shipped the fix in its May 2026 Critical Security Patch Update. Organizations running Oracle EBS in the affected version range who have not applied the May CPU should treat this as an emergency — the vulnerability enables complete system compromise with no authentication required.

### Microsoft July 2026 Patch Tuesday: Record 622 CVEs, Two Actively Exploited Zero-Days
**Source:** The Hacker News / SecurityWeek | [Read more](https://thehackernews.com/2026/07/microsoft-patches-record-622-flaws.html)

Microsoft's July Patch Tuesday addressed 622 CVEs — more than triple June's volume — driven in part by MDASH, Microsoft's internal multi-model agentic security scanner. Two zero-days are under active exploitation: CVE-2026-56164 (SharePoint Server, unauthenticated network privilege escalation) and CVE-2026-56155 (AD FS, authenticated local privilege escalation to administrator). A third zero-day, CVE-2026-50661 (BitLocker bypass, requires physical access), was publicly disclosed prior to the patch. A separate JWT authentication bypass (CVE-2026-55040, found by Rapid7 Labs) chains to an unpatched RCE in the same product; Microsoft is deferring the RCE fix to August's Patch Tuesday, leaving a partial exposure window. SharePoint and AD FS should be patched on an emergency basis given confirmed in-the-wild exploitation.

## Industry & Trends

### Apple Sues OpenAI Over Trade Secret Theft: Ex-Employees Allegedly Brought Hardware Designs to Interviews
**Source:** CNBC / TechCrunch | [Read more](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/10/apple-openai-lawsuit-trade-secrets.html)

Apple filed suit against OpenAI in federal court in Northern California on July 10, alleging systematic trade secret theft designed to accelerate OpenAI's consumer hardware efforts. The complaint alleges OpenAI's hardware chief — a former Apple VP — directed job candidates still employed at Apple to bring physical hardware components to interviews as "show and tell." A named defendant, former Apple senior systems electrical engineer Chang Liu, is alleged to have downloaded confidential technical documents to an unreturned company laptop before joining OpenAI. The suit is a sharp reversal from the 2024 ChatGPT-Siri partnership, which Apple has since replaced with a Google Gemini integration. The case will scrutinize the blurry line between competitive recruiting and trade secret misappropriation — a risk pattern common across AI talent pipelines in 2026.

### UN Launches AI for Good Global Commission; Global Governance Race Accelerates
**Source:** UN News / CFR | [Read more](https://news.un.org/en/story/2026/07/1167862)

The UN Global Dialogue on AI Governance convened in Geneva on July 6-7, 2026, with governments, tech companies, academics, and civil society debating international frameworks for a technology advancing faster than regulation. The UN launched the AI for Good Global Commission — bringing together C-suite AI executives and heads of state — to forge binding global rules. National approaches are rapidly diverging: China's agent regulations took effect July 15 (see above), the EU AI Act full enforcement begins August 2, and US states are enacting individual frameworks including mandatory safety audits (Illinois) and new liability regimes. For software teams, the convergence of multiple overlapping regulatory timelines in H2 2026 means AI governance compliance is a near-term engineering requirement rather than a deferred legal concern.
